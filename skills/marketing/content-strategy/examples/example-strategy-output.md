# the example coaching business Content Strategy (Example Output)

This is the actual output of the content-strategy skill run for Jared Langley at Early Operator stage transitioning to Stable Solo. Use as the reference shape when running the skill for a new user.

```
CONTENT STRATEGY, Jared Langley, Stable Solo ($5K MRR)

ICP anchor channel: LinkedIn personal (jaredmurraybruce slug)
Secondary channel (repurpose): Threads + LinkedIn company page
Owned channels: Newsletter (MailerLite lead-gen assessment subscribers group) + Blog (Groundwork at jaredlangley.cc/blog)

ICP description:
B2B SaaS frontline sales managers, under 18 months in role, 50 to 500 employees,
US/CA/UK/AU. Just got promoted from individual contributor. Feels like they're
faking it. Trying to figure out how to lead the team they were on last week.

Pillars:
1. Presence, Stop reactive leadership. Show up fully in every moment.
2. Performance, Execute with intention behind the effort. No more thrashing.
3. Wholeness, Lead the whole person. You included.

Cadence:
- LinkedIn personal: 2 posts/week (Mon + Thu)
- LinkedIn company page: 2 posts/week (Tue + Fri carousels)
- Threads: 5 posts/week (Mon-Fri 12 pm PT)
- Instagram: 2 posts/week (Tue carousel + Thu Reels)
- TikTok: 1 post/week (Thu evening cinematic)
- Newsletter: 1/week Sunday (lead-gen assessment subscribers group)
- Blog: 1 essay/week Sunday (Groundwork)

Total: ~12 posts/week + 1 newsletter + 1 blog essay.

Time budget: 6-8 hrs/week
- 1.5 hrs Monday morning: blog essay first draft
- 30 min Tuesday morning: essay finalize + LinkedIn pull
- 30 min Wednesday morning: Threads chain + IG carousel
- 30 min Thursday morning: short-form video script + quote graphic
- 1 hr Friday morning: schedule next week into Buffer + native schedulers
- 30 min Sunday morning: newsletter top-frame + send

Topic seed list (10 ICP-language phrases from real client calls):
- "I'm always reacting, never planning"
- "My boss's one-on-ones got worse after I got promoted"
- "I don't know how to coach the rep who used to outsell me"
- "I'm working 60 hours and my number is still not safe"
- "I can't tell if I'm a good manager or just a busy one"
- "Everyone wants something from me and nothing for me"
- "I came in early and stayed late and we still missed the quarter"
- "I have one-on-ones but they're status reads, not coaching"
- "I'm scared to give feedback that lands as criticism"
- "I keep saying I'll start that gym/journal/sleep habit when Q1 closes"

Pillar rotation rule:
Each week's blog essay tags to one pillar. Rotation Presence → Performance → Wholeness
→ Presence (3-week cycle). Social posts within the week each tag to one pillar with
no rotation rule (lets the essay dictate the week's tilt).

Revenue tag:
- Direct ($$): LinkedIn personal posts that mention lead-gen assessment or free intro call
- Leverage ($$): Newsletter (compounds owned audience), blog (compounds SEO and AI citation)
- Distraction risk: TikTok cinematic without ROI tracking, review at quarter end

CTA hierarchy on every piece:
1. Primary: lead-gen assessment (lead magnet, /vibe-check)
2. Secondary: free intro call (cal.com/langleycoaching/clarity-session), for warm readers only
3. Soft: Newsletter signup (every blog footer, every newsletter forwarding section)
```

## Notes on this output

The output above assumes the user has already validated:

- LinkedIn personal as the anchor (10+ inbound DMs/month from posts in W17-W21)
- Pillar set locked through positioning work in 2024-2025
- Offer locked at $X,XXX Foundation (April 2026 lock)
- ICP locked at B2B SaaS sales managers (April 2026 outbound work confirmed)

Without those locks, the output would be flagged "incomplete, return to positioning/offer-design first."

## What changed from the prior strategy

Earlier strategy (April 2026 doc) included:

- Daily Threads + 2-3 IG posts/week + LinkedIn company page + TikTok cinematic + personal LI

That was 10 posts/week without a spine. Production was ad-hoc. The user was inventing every post.

Locked strategy (May 2026) adds:

- Sunday blog essay as the spine
- 6 derivatives per essay (newsletter + LinkedIn long-form + Threads chain + IG carousel + short-form video + quote graphic)
- 1 reflective Threads post mid-week outside the spine

Hours dropped from 10 to 6 to 8. Output stayed the same volume. Quality went up because every piece was on-thesis.

## When this output would change

- If LinkedIn personal stopped driving DMs (4 weeks of zero), re-anchor
- If lead-gen assessment signups stayed under 20/month at $5K MRR, re-test lead magnet
- If TikTok showed 0 attribution after 3 months, kill the channel
- If the newsletter passed 1000 subscribers, upgrade MailerLite to paid tier, add an automation layer

Quarterly review is the trigger to revise. Daily output noise is not.

## How to adapt this output for a different user

Run the 4-step build with their assessment data. Swap:

- ICP anchor channel (per `icp-channel-map.md`)
- Pillars (per `pillar-test.md`, must pass all 4 Qs)
- Cadence (per stage in the SKILL.md)
- Topic seed list (must be ICP-language, not industry-speak)
- Revenue tags (per `revenue-mapper`)
- Time budget (within stage cap, respecting family-floor)

Do not copy the Langley pillars (Presence/Performance/Wholeness) to another user. Those are specific to Foundation coaching. Generate fresh pillars from the user's offer + ICP.
