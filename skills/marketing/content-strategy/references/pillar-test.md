# Pillar Test

4 questions to validate a candidate pillar before locking it into the user's content strategy. If a pillar fails 2 or more, drop it. Pillars are commitments. The user will spend 50+ weeks talking about each.

## The 4 questions

### 1. Does it name an outcome, worldview, or shift, not a topic?

Topics are too generic. "Sales" is a topic. "Sales is taught wrong" is a pillar.

| Bad pillar (topic) | Good pillar (worldview) |
|---|---|
| Sales | Sales is taught wrong |
| Pricing | Charge what your work is worth |
| Productivity | Output beats hours |
| Leadership | Lead the whole person |
| Marketing | Stop chasing leads, build a stage |

A pillar must contain a claim, not a category. The user is taking a side.

### 2. Does it ladder to the offer?

Trace the line from pillar to a paid outcome. If you can't connect them in one sentence, the pillar is brand fluff.

Examples:

- Pillar "Lead the whole person" → Foundation coaching package → ladder: "When leaders perform on paper but feel empty, they hire me for a 90-day reset." Connects.
- Pillar "Best productivity apps of 2026" → Coaching package → ladder: "?" Doesn't connect. Cut.

### 3. Does the ICP recognize their own problem in it?

Read the pillar to an actual ICP and ask, "Does this describe a real problem you have?" If they shrug or rephrase it as something different, the pillar is not in their language.

Pillars in ICP language pass. Pillars in industry-speak fail.

### 4. Can the user write 30 pieces on it in 6 months without repeating themselves?

A pillar is a 12-month commitment minimum. If the user can only think of 5 things to say about it, it's not a pillar, it's a topic.

Test: ask the user to list 10 sub-angles on the pillar in 5 minutes. If they hit 10, the pillar has depth. If they stall at 4, the pillar is too narrow.

## Common candidate pillars and verdicts

### "Marketing for coaches"

- Outcome? No, it's a topic. FAIL Q1.
- Cut, or reframe: "Coaches who post too much never get clients."

### "Building in public"

- Outcome? Yes, it's a stance.
- Ladders to offer? Sometimes. If the user sells "how to build in public" courses, yes. If they sell tax services, no.
- ICP recognizes? In tech yes, in services no.
- 30 pieces in 6 months? For an indie SaaS dev, easy.
- Verdict: depends on user.

### "The whole leader"

- Outcome? Yes, a worldview.
- Ladders to Foundation coaching offer? Yes (the example coaching business).
- ICP (B2B sales managers) recognizes? Yes after one conversation.
- 30 pieces in 6 months? Yes, the 3 pillars (Presence/Performance/Wholeness) alone give endless angles.
- Verdict: PASS.

### "Bootstrapping a SaaS to $10K MRR"

- Outcome? Yes.
- Ladders to offer? Yes if the user sells a course or coaching on this. No if they sell a different product.
- ICP recognizes? Yes for indie devs.
- 30 pieces in 6 months? Yes.
- Verdict: PASS for indie SaaS coaches/teachers.

### "Real estate tips"

- Outcome? No, generic topic.
- FAIL Q1.
- Cut, or reframe: "How to sell a home that's been on the market too long."

### "Mindset"

- Outcome? Too vague.
- FAIL Q1.
- Cut, or reframe: "Mindset work without action is a dopamine hit, not change."

### "Presence"

- Outcome? Yes (a worldview about how leaders show up).
- Ladders to offer? Yes (Foundation pillar 1).
- ICP recognizes? Yes (sales managers know the panic of reactive leadership).
- 30 pieces in 6 months? Yes.
- Verdict: PASS.

## Pillar set composition

Once each candidate pillar passes, check the set:

- 3 to 5 pillars total
- Each pillar covers a distinct slice of the offer (no overlap)
- Together they cover the full offer (no gaps)
- One pillar is the "contrarian" pillar (where the user takes a controversial stance)
- One pillar is the "personal" pillar (where the user shares their own story)
- Two to three pillars are "framework" pillars (where the user teaches their method)

Example, the example coaching business:

| Pillar | Type | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Presence | Framework | Stop reactive leadership |
| Performance | Framework | Execute with intention |
| Wholeness | Personal + contrarian | Lead the whole person, including yourself |

3 pillars, covers the flagship offer framework, includes a contrarian + personal layer (Wholeness pushes back on "leave your feelings at the door" culture).

## When to update pillars

Quarterly review. Pillars are 12-month minimum commitments, but if data shows:

- One pillar gets 5x the engagement of another → expand that pillar
- One pillar gets no engagement after 3 months → kill it (test 1 replacement candidate)
- A new pillar emerges from a recurring DM theme → add it as a 4th, test for one quarter

Don't churn pillars more than once per quarter. The audience needs to learn what the user stands for, and that takes repetition.
