# Cross-Post Social Spine (From One Newsletter)

How a single Sunday newsletter generates 6 social posts for the week. This is the repurposing spine in action.

Source issue: "Three reactive triggers I caught myself doing this week" (Sunday May 24, 2026 newsletter, see `example-sunday-issue.md`).

## The week's social spine

| Day | Time | Channel | Piece | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sunday | 8 pm PT | Blog publish | Full essay at jaredlangley.cc/blog/three-reactive-triggers | Essay |
| Sunday | 9 am PT | Newsletter | "Three reactive triggers I caught myself doing this week" | Newsletter |
| Monday | 12 pm PT | Threads chain (5 posts) | Numbered triggers + the pause | Body beats 1-3 + land |
| Monday | 7 pm PT | LinkedIn personal | Long-form pull from frame + first trigger | Frame + beat 1 |
| Tuesday | 9 am PT | Instagram carousel (8 slides) | Visual breakdown of 3 triggers + pause | Body beats + land |
| Wednesday | 12 pm PT | Threads quote post | "Most of the time it's the second one." | Land line |
| Thursday | 9 am PT | LinkedIn company page | Carousel repost (same as IG) | IG carousel |
| Thursday | 5 pm PT | TikTok / IG Reels | 75-sec script: "You think you're a busy manager" | Hook + beat 2 + land |
| Friday | 12 pm PT | Threads reflection | Mid-week noticing post (off-spine, real-time) | New observation |

Total: 7 social pieces + 1 newsletter + 1 blog essay from one Saturday morning's writing.

## Post 1: Monday Threads chain

**Post 1/5:**

> Most new sales managers think the problem is time management.
>
> It's not.
>
> Three triggers run their week and they don't see it. Thread.

**Post 2/5:**

> Trigger one: the morning Slack scroll.
>
> You tell yourself it's a "quick check." It's 23 minutes. You come out knowing what everyone needs from you, and not what you need from the day.
>
> The scroll set the agenda. You just executed against it.

**Post 3/5:**

> Trigger two: the interrupt 1:1.
>
> Rep brings a fire. You solve it on the spot. They walk out with the fire put out and no plan.
>
> They walk back into the next fire by Wednesday.

**Post 4/5:**

> Trigger three: the 4 pm deal review.
>
> Friday afternoon, you review pipeline. The meeting is about a deal that wasn't urgent at noon and is urgent by 4:45.
>
> You give it 30 minutes that should have gone to next week's prep.
>
> Monday comes. The deal closed. The prep didn't.

**Post 5/5:**

> The fix isn't a calendar trick.
>
> The fix is the pause. Three to five seconds between trigger and response. Long enough to ask: is this the work I planned, or the work that just showed up?
>
> Pick one trigger this week. Kill it for five days. See what shows up in the space.
>
> Full essay: jaredlangley.cc/blog/three-reactive-triggers

**Voice check:** clean.
**Time to draft:** 15 minutes (the body is already written, just chunking).

## Post 2: Monday LinkedIn personal (long-form pull)

> Saturday morning. Coffee. Notebook. Kid asleep upstairs. The hour I write best is the hour I almost trade for one more meeting every week. I almost did it again yesterday.
>
> I noticed it because I'd been watching myself all week for the same pattern in other domains. Three places I was reacting when I thought I was leading.
>
> The first was the morning Slack scroll. I'd told myself it was a "quick check." It was 23 minutes. I came out of it knowing what everyone else needed from me, and not what I needed from the day.
>
> The pattern is the same in each. Something noisy showed up. I responded. The response felt productive because I'd done something. But the day had been hijacked.
>
> The fix isn't a calendar trick. The fix is the pause. Three to five seconds between trigger and response. Long enough to ask: is this the work I planned to do, or the work that just showed up?
>
> Most of the time it's the second one. Sometimes that's correct. Often it's not.
>
> The full essay covers the other two triggers and the 5-day reset: jaredlangley.cc/blog/three-reactive-triggers
>
> What's the one trigger you'd pick? Reply or DM.

**Character count:** ~1450 (within LinkedIn's "see more" optimal range).
**Voice check:** clean.

## Post 7: Friday Threads reflection (off-spine, real-time)

This one is NOT from the essay. It's a real-time reflection the user notices on Friday.

> Caught myself doing trigger 1 again at 8:47 this morning. I'd told myself, "just a quick check."
>
> Closed the app at 8:50.
>
> The work I'd planned was sitting on the desk. It's still there. I just sat down to it.

**Voice check:** clean.
**Pattern:** mid-week real-time post breaks the spine. Highest engagement of the week. Specifically because it's not from the essay.

## What this spine does NOT include

- One-off reply DMs and comments (budget 30 minutes a day, not on the spine)
- Live-streams or webinars (different week)
- Real-time event reactions (off-spine, write fresh)
- Guest posts or podcast appearances (different week)

The spine is the week's predictable content. Everything else is opportunistic.
