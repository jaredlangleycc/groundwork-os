# Visual Direction Guide

Brand-aligned ad visual conventions. What to refuse. The squint test. Asset sources for solopreneurs without designers.

## The brand alignment principle

Every ad visual carries the user's brand. Ads that look like generic SaaS templates kill trust before the headline lands. The visual is a credential.

For the example coaching business:

- Cream (#F2EDE6) or deep teal (#0A1F20) background
- Gold (#D4A96A) accent
- Archivo Black headline (uppercase, tight tracking, -0.03em)
- Manrope body (regular weight)
- Single focal element per visual
- No emoji
- Brand "L" mark in corner (optional, small)

For other users, swap in their brand palette + fonts from voice-rules and brand-strategist outputs.

## The squint test

Look at the visual at 50% size, half-squinted. If you can't make out:

- The headline
- The brand
- The single key visual element

Then the visual is too busy. Cut.

Ads compete with 200+ pieces of feed content per scroll session. The squint test is the only honest preview of how the ad will perform.

## Visual archetypes that work

### 1. Quote card (text on brand background)

A single quotable line on a brand-aligned card. Minimal visual.

Example:

> "Most of the time it's the second one."
>
>, Jared Langley

Background: cream. Text: deep teal Archivo Black. "  Jared Langley" attribution in smaller Manrope.

Best for: thought-leadership campaigns, top-of-funnel awareness.

### 2. Question card (text-only, large)

A single question that prompts a click.

Example:

> WHICH PILLAR
> IS CARRYING
> THE WEIGHT?

Background: cream. Text: deep teal. Each line in Archivo Black.

Best for: lead-magnet drive (the lead-gen assessment answers the question).

### 3. Framework card (numbered list)

A short numbered framework on a card.

Example:

> THE THREE PILLARS
>
> 01. PRESENCE
> 02. PERFORMANCE
> 03. WHOLENESS

Background: cream. Numbers gold. Pillar names deep teal Archivo Black.

Best for: framework-led traffic, intro to the methodology.

### 4. Creator-led portrait

The user's photo, brand-treated, with a single line.

Example:

> Jared Langley, ICF-accredited coach
>
> Photo: brand-aligned portrait, cream background or studio black

Best for: trust-building, retargeting (warm audiences who've engaged but not converted).

### 5. Before/after stat card

A specific data point or transformation.

Example:

> 60-hour weeks → 45-hour weeks
> Still hit the number.

Background: cream. Numbers gold Archivo Black. Body deep teal Manrope.

Best for: bottom-of-funnel, conversion-focused.

### 6. Testimonial card (short)

A real client quote with name + role.

Example:

> "I came in expecting tactics. I left with a different way of seeing my week."
>
>, Sam
> VP People, Series D SaaS

Background: cream. Quote in deep teal Manrope, slightly italic. Attribution in smaller Manrope.

Best for: warm retargeting, conversion.

## Visual archetypes to refuse

### Stock photo "diverse smiling team in office"

Refuse. The audience sees this 10x per scroll. Zero differentiation.

### Stock photo "person at desk holding chin / thinking"

Refuse. Generic, AI-style, low-trust.

### Person standing in front of whiteboard pointing at marker drawing

Refuse. Stock-photo trope, signals AI/template ad.

### Overlay arrows pointing at the person's face

Refuse. Cheap clickbait look.

### Excessive emoji + caps + multiple colors

Refuse. Looks like AliExpress dropshipper ad.

### AI-generated stock illustrations

Refuse if generated. AI illustrations are recognizable in 2026 and erode trust.

### Cluttered backgrounds with multiple focal elements

Refuse. One focal element per visual.

### Brand-mismatched assets

If the user's brand palette is teal + gold and the ad has a hot-pink background, refuse. Brand cohesion across organic and paid matters.

## Visual production sources

### Solopreneur-tier (free or cheap)

- **Canva Free / Pro:** brand kit, template-driven, fast
- **Figma Free:** more flexible, requires design skills, free for solos
- **Buffer / Hypefury creative tools:** built into scheduler, limited
- **User's existing brand template:** if `brand-strategist` has produced a template, reuse it for every ad

### Photography sources

- **User's own brand photoshoot:** ideal, ~$500-2000 once
- **Unsplash / Pexels (curated):** if the user must use stock, choose abstract/object/setting shots, NOT people. People-stock-photos kill trust. (See memory `feedback_diverse-stock-photos.md` for the warning.)
- **AI generators (Midjourney, Flux):** acceptable for abstract / background visuals only, NEVER for people or face-similar visuals
- **Brand assets folder:** the user's existing brand visuals (the example coaching business: `brand-assets/` with 17 PNGs and a Vibe Wave Overlay tool at `brand-assets/vibe-wave-overlay.html`)

### Video production tools

- **Phone + natural light:** the highest-converting "video ad" for personal brands is the user on camera, phone, soft natural light
- **Descript:** captions, edit, transcript-based editing
- **CapCut:** mobile-first editor, free, good captions
- **Final Cut / Premiere:** if user has skills
- **OpenMontage / Remotion:** programmatic video for branded card animations

### Asset organization

User should keep an "ad-creative-bank" folder with:

- Brand-aligned card templates (Figma or Canva)
- Approved portraits (cropped, brand-treated)
- Quote graphics from past posts
- Testimonial cards (per testimonial)
- Logo + brand mark variants

When generating new ad creative, pull from this bank first. Don't recreate every time.

## Static vs video decision

| Variable | Static | Video |
|---|---|---|
| Production time | 15 to 45 min per variant | 30 min to 4 hours per variant |
| Production cost | Free (templates) | Phone-recorded free, edited cheap |
| Best for | Awareness, retargeting, framework | Hook-driven, personal brand, creator-led |
| Fatigue rate | Faster (2 to 4 weeks) | Slower (3 to 6 weeks) |
| Conversion rate | Lower CTR, higher CVR (qualified) | Higher CTR, sometimes lower CVR (volume) |
| Test variant count | Easier to test 5+ variants | Practical to test 2-3 variants |

Default for Early Operator: static.

Stable Solo: static + 1 video baseline.

Scaling Solo: 50/50 mix, possibly more video as creative budget allows.

## Aspect ratio per placement

| Placement | Aspect ratio | Dimensions |
|---|---|---|
| Meta feed (square) | 1:1 | 1080x1080 |
| Meta feed (portrait) | 4:5 | 1080x1350 |
| Meta Stories / Reels | 9:16 | 1080x1920 |
| LinkedIn feed (landscape) | 1.91:1 | 1200x627 |
| LinkedIn feed (square) | 1:1 | 1200x1200 |
| LinkedIn video (square) | 1:1 | 1200x1200 |
| LinkedIn video (vertical) | 9:16 | 1080x1920 |

Pick the placement first. Don't design a 1:1 and then "make it work" for Stories.

## Captioning rules

For video:

- Captions burned in (not auto-cap, not subtitles file)
- Sans-serif font (Manrope Bold for Langley)
- White text on subtle dark drop shadow (legible on any background)
- Bottom-center or top-center placement (not over the speaker's face)
- 2 to 5 words per caption frame
- Sync to speech rhythm (one phrase, one cap)

For static:

- Headline in brand font, size readable at 50% zoom
- Body text under 30 words on image
- Don't compete with visual focal element

## Brand mark placement

- Small brand mark in corner: OK
- Logo at the bottom of every static ad: OK
- Logo splashed across the center: refuse
- "Sponsored by [brand]" overlay: refuse (Meta/LinkedIn already labels ads)

## Visual style by stage

### Pre-Rev Builder

REFUSE ads entirely. No visual work needed.

### Early Operator

- Quote cards (lowest production cost)
- Framework cards
- Creator-led single portrait

Skip video until production cost can be amortized.

### Stable Solo

- All 6 archetypes available
- 50/50 static/video
- Brand template should be locked

### Scaling Solo

- All archetypes
- Possible animation (subtle, brand-aligned)
- Consider contractor for video editing (per the spine pattern, hand off the cut)

## Refresh signals

Refresh the visual when:

- CPC has crept 30%+ from week 1 baseline
- CTR has dropped 40%+ from week 1
- Frequency is over 4 (saturation)
- The visual is over 6 weeks old AND a fresh variant beat it in a test

Don't refresh just because. Refresh on data.

## Visual decision tree (quick)

```
Is the user at Early Operator+ with validated organic?
  No → REFUSE ad creative entirely. Use organic.
  Yes → continue.

Is there a Brand template?
  No → Build template first (route to `brand-strategist`).
  Yes → continue.

Is there a winning organic post?
  No → REFUSE, no baseline to model on.
  Yes → continue.

Which archetype matches the campaign goal?
  Awareness → quote or question card
  Trust → creator-led portrait or testimonial card
  Conversion → framework or before/after stat card
  Retargeting → testimonial card or creator-led portrait

Static or video?
  First test → static (lower production)
  Hook-driven → video
  Personal brand → video

Squint test pass?
  No → simplify, cut
  Yes → ship
```
