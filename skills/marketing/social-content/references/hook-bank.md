# Hook Bank

50+ hook archetypes with examples. Organized by post type. Voice-scan before use.

## Hook archetypes that work

### 1. The counterintuitive claim

Pattern: name the common belief, then flip it.

- "Most new sales managers think the problem is time management. It's not."
- "Stop telling new managers to 'be a leader.' The advice is too vague to act on."
- "Your calendar is lying to you about what matters."
- "The fix isn't a calendar trick."
- "Your one-on-ones aren't coaching. They're status reads."

When to use: framework posts, teaching posts, hot takes.

### 2. The specific moment

Pattern: name a place, time, sensory detail, or quoted moment.

- "Saturday morning. Coffee. Notebook. Kid asleep upstairs."
- "Yesterday at my kid's soccer game I caught myself answering Slack on the sideline."
- "A client said this on Friday: 'I have to read my reps' Slack to know what they actually mean.'"
- "8:47 am. I'd told myself it was a quick check."
- "October. First fire in the woodstove. Writing this."

When to use: personal posts, newsletter-style social, openers for narrative threads.

### 3. The number anchor

Pattern: state a count that creates structure for the reader.

- "Three reactive triggers I caught myself doing this week."
- "Five questions I ask every new manager in their first 90 days."
- "Two reasons your one-on-ones got worse after the promotion."
- "One sentence I rewrote 6 times this week."
- "Four weeks. No new clients. One pattern."

When to use: list posts, framework posts, structured teaching.

### 4. The named example

Pattern: open with a real person or company.

- "A client. B2B SaaS sales manager. 8 months in. Working 60-hour weeks."
- "Patagonia gave away the company. Yvon Chouinard didn't make a speech about it."
- "Indra Nooyi wrote 400 letters to the parents of her senior executives. Hand-written."
- "A friend texted at 11:47 pm: 'I just told my CEO I'm out.'"

When to use: case study posts, story posts, proof-of-concept.

### 5. The question (used sparingly)

Pattern: ask one question the reader can't ignore.

- "When did your week start being about other people?"
- "What did you do last week that you'll still be proud of in 12 months?"
- "Where in your week are you doing $200/hr work for $20/hr pay?"
- "What does your team know about you that you haven't told them?"

When to use: reflective posts, end-of-week prompts. Don't open every post with a question; it tires the reader.

### 6. The pattern interrupt

Pattern: name what most people are doing, then dismiss it.

- "Stop telling new managers to 'be a leader.'"
- "Delete every 'best practice' article you saved this month."
- "Quit posting on LinkedIn for 30 days. See what happens."
- "Cancel your 1-on-1 with your manager this week. Watch what happens."

When to use: contrarian posts. Use sparingly; one or two per quarter, not weekly.

### 7. The before/after frame

Pattern: state where you were, where you are now.

- "Two years ago I worked 60 hours and missed my number. Last quarter we hit it in 45."
- "Three months ago this client was stuck. Today we shipped her first asynchronous week."
- "A year ago I didn't have a newsletter. Today the list is 1,200 and growing."

When to use: case study posts, milestone posts, proof posts. Use specific numbers.

### 8. The data point

Pattern: open with one number, then unpack.

- "23 minutes. That's how long my 'quick check' on Slack lasted yesterday morning."
- "0.6% conversion. The newsletter has been sending to 1,400 people. That's the number that matters."
- "8 hours. Average new sales manager's reactive time per week. Mine was 14 last month."

When to use: analytical posts, audit posts.

### 9. The quote

Pattern: open with a real quote from the user's life or work.

- "'I have to read their Slack to know what they actually mean.'"
- "'I came in early. I stayed late. We still missed the quarter.'"
- "'I don't know how to coach the rep who used to outsell me.'"
- "'When can I just be myself at work?'"

When to use: client-call insight posts, ICP-language posts.

### 10. The reversal

Pattern: state a common goal, reframe what it actually requires.

- "You don't need more leads. You need fewer, better ones."
- "You don't need a content plan. You need one piece you'd defend in person."
- "You don't need a calendar trick. You need a pause."

When to use: framework posts, advice posts.

Note: this can drift into "X, not Y" antipattern. Rewrite affirmatively when possible.

Bad: "You need fewer leads, not more."
Good: "You don't need more leads. You need fewer, better ones."

## Hooks to refuse

### AI tells

- "Imagine if you could..."
- "Picture this..."
- "What if I told you..."
- "Today I learned..."
- "Quick story..."
- "Let me share something with you..."
- "I want to talk about..."
- "Today I want to share..."

These signal AI authorship in 2026. Refuse.

### Generic openers

- "Most people don't know this, but..."
- "Here's the truth about..."
- "Let's talk about..."
- "The secret to [thing] is..."
- "If you want to [outcome], you need to [advice]"

Bland. Refuse.

### Engagement bait

- "Repost if you agree"
- "Comment YES if you've felt this"
- "Tag someone who needs to see this"
- "Like if you've been there"

Punished by algorithm. Refuse.

### Hashtags as hooks

- "#Mindset #Monday #Motivation"
- "#StartupLife #FoundersJourney"

Refuse opening posts with hashtags.

### Personal slogans / brand affirmations

- "I'm on a mission to..."
- "We help [audience] [verb] [outcome]"
- "My goal is to..."

Refuse. The reader doesn't care about the user's mission until the user earns it.

| LinkedIn company | Framework or testimonial | 6 to 12 words |
| Threads | Punchy claim or moment | 4 to 10 words |

| IG carousel slide 1 | Bold claim, on visual | 4 to 8 words |
| TikTok | Direct claim, no preamble | 3 to 8 words first second |
| X | Punchy claim or pattern interrupt | 4 to 12 words |

## Hook calibration by archetype

Different user archetypes carry different hook styles natively:

### Operator archetype

- Direct claims
- Numbers
- "Stop / Start / Cut" patterns

### Storyteller archetype

- Specific moments
- Named examples
- Quotes from real life

### Challenger archetype

- Pattern interrupts
- Counterintuitive claims
- Provocations

### Mentor archetype

- Questions
- Frameworks
- Reflective prompts

### Curator archetype

- Data points
- Observations
- "Here's what I noticed"

When generating hooks, match the user's voice archetype from voice-rules.

## Hook A/B testing patterns

When the user is at Stable Solo or above, test hooks systematically.

| Variant A | Variant B | Hypothesis |
|---|---|---|
| Counterintuitive claim | Specific moment | Authority vs intimacy |
| Number anchor | Named example | Structure vs story |
| Hot take | Reflective question | Sharp vs warm |
| Quote | Data point | Voice vs proof |

Test on 4 to 6 posts per variant before drawing conclusions. Sample size matters.

## Hook lift indicators

A hook is working when:

- LinkedIn: 5x typical impressions in first hour
- Threads: 3+ replies in first 20 minutes
- IG carousel: save rate 5%+ on impressions
- TikTok: completion rate 50%+ on first 30 seconds

A hook is failing when:

- LinkedIn: <1x typical impressions in first hour
- Threads: 0 replies in first hour
- IG: scroll-past rate >90%
- TikTok: drop-off in first 3 seconds

Diagnose the hook before the body. The body can be perfect and the hook still kills it.
