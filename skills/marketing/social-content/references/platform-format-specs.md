# Platform Format Specs

Per-platform spec sheet. Character counts, hashtag rules, post-time patterns, visual conventions. Use as the source of truth when drafting per platform.

## LinkedIn personal feed

### Character counts

- Hard cap: 3000 characters
- "See more" clip at ~200 characters (mobile) / ~270 characters (desktop)
- Optimal range: 1200 to 1800 characters
- Headline cap: 220 characters

### Format rules

- First line carries the hook. The user is writing two posts: the first line (which earns the click), and the rest (which earns the read).
- Paragraph breaks every 1 to 2 sentences. Walls of text die in the LinkedIn feed.
- White space matters. Each paragraph should look like 3 to 6 lines tall on phone.
- Line breaks within paragraphs are fine. Use to slow the reader down.
- No emojis (Langley convention). Some users use emojis sparingly as bullet markers. Refuse decorative emojis.

### Hashtag rules

- 0 to 3 hashtags
- Place at the end, on a single line, after the body
- Specific hashtags beat broad ones (#salesleadership > #leadership)
- Skip if not adding signal

### Post-time patterns

- Best: Tuesday to Thursday 7 to 9 am audience-local
- Second best: lunch (12 to 1 pm audience-local)
- Avoid: Monday morning (full inboxes), Friday afternoon (checked out)
- Weekends: low impressions for B2B audiences, OK for B2C

### Engagement mechanics

- Comments in first 60 minutes matter most
- Reply to every comment in first 24 hours
- Long replies (multi-sentence) extend post lifespan
- Edit window: don't edit after 30 minutes (algorithm flag)

## LinkedIn company page

### Character counts

- Same as personal feed
- Headline cap same

### Format rules

- Tone shifts to brand voice (less "I", more "we observe" or framework-led)
- Logos and brand visuals OK (different from personal)
- Carousels work well on company pages

### Hashtag rules

- 0 to 3, same as personal

### Reach reality

- Company page organic reach is roughly 1/10 of personal feed in 2026
- Sponsor / boost campaigns work for company pages
- Best use: reposts of personal-feed bangers, testimonials, frameworks

## Threads

### Character counts

- Hard cap: 500 characters per post
- Chain length: 3 to 7 posts is the sweet spot
- Longer chains can work for narrative storytelling

### Format rules

- Conversational tone. Less polished than LinkedIn.
- First post is hook + frame. Last post offers asset or reply prompt.
- Emojis fine for users who use them. Refuse for Langley (per brand convention).
- Don't over-thread. 5 to 7 posts max for non-narrative content.

### Hashtag rules

- 0 to 2 hashtags
- Threads is search-light, hashtag value is lower than IG
- Use only if topically relevant

### Post-time patterns

- Best: 12 pm PT (audience scroll-during-lunch pattern)
- Second best: 7 to 9 pm PT
- Daily cadence works (Langley: noon PT, 7 days/week)

### Reach mechanics

- Replies extend reach more than likes
- Reposts get amplified in algorithm
- Engagement bait ("comment X if you agree") is penalized

## Instagram

### Character counts

- Caption cap: 2200 characters
- Optimal caption: 100 to 300 characters
- First 125 characters before "more" truncation

### Format rules

- Visual leads. Caption supports.
- Caption can be longer if it's narrative + valuable
- Line breaks via empty lines (no @ workarounds needed)
- Emojis acceptable in moderation (Langley refuses, others fine)

### IG Carousel specs

- 1 to 10 slides
- Square (1080x1080) or portrait (1080x1350)
- Slide 1: hook + visual
- Slide 2 to N-1: one idea per slide
- Last slide: CTA or summary
- Save-driven engagement (saves > likes for compounding)

### IG Reel specs

- 15 to 90 seconds (60 to 75 is sweet spot)
- 9:16 vertical
- Hook in first 1 second
- Captions burned in (most viewers watch muted)
- Native sound or trending audio (lift)
- 1 idea per Reel

### Hashtag rules

- 5 to 10 hashtags
- Mix broad (#leadership) + niche (#newsalesmanager) + branded (#langleycoaching)
- Place in first comment OR end of caption
- Refuse 30-hashtag spam

### Post-time patterns

- Best: 9 am or 6 pm audience-local
- Reels: late evening drives most reach
- Avoid: Sunday afternoon (low engagement window)

## TikTok

### Length specs

- Hard cap: 10 minutes (but useless for thought-leadership)
- Sweet spot: 60 to 75 seconds for thought-leadership
- Sub-15 seconds for tight one-claim posts

### Format rules

- 9:16 vertical
- Hook in 0 to 3 seconds (algorithm cuts at first scroll)
- One beat per script
- Captions burned in (auto-captions cleaned up at minimum)
- Trending audio for lift, original audio for trust
- Native feel beats over-produced

### Hashtag rules

- 3 to 5 hashtags
- Specific niches: #newsalesmanager, #salesmanagement, #leadershipdevelopment
- Avoid broad: #business, #motivation, #success

### Post-time patterns

- 6 to 9 am audience-local (commute scroll)
- 7 to 11 pm audience-local (evening scroll)
- Friday + Saturday evening highest engagement for B2C niches

### Visual conventions (Langley)

- Black background
- Captions in TOP + BOTTOM thirds (not center)
- Manrope Bold cream captions
- Voice-amplitude visual or branded card
- No subject face for branded story posts

## X (formerly Twitter)

### Character counts

- 280 characters per post (free)
- 25000 characters for X Premium subs (long posts)
- Threads: 5 to 12 posts

### Format rules

- Punchier than LinkedIn or Threads
- First post is the claim
- Each post in thread = one beat
- Last post offers asset

### Hashtag rules

- 0 to 2 hashtags
- Search value low in 2026
- Use only when joining a conversation

### Post-time patterns

- 7 to 9 am ET (US east coast scroll)
- 12 pm ET (lunch scroll)
- 8 to 11 pm ET (evening)

### Reach mechanics

- Quote tweets > replies > likes for amplification
- Threads get pinned attention from the author's followers
- Algorithm favors recent engagement (first hour critical)

## Cross-platform repurposing rules

- Don't post identical text across platforms (algorithm flags as duplicate, low reach)
- Adapt format and length per platform spec
- Same idea, different shape
- Different first lines per platform (hook must match platform culture)
