## What works in this carousel

- **One idea per slide.** Reader can swipe at their own pace.
- **Slide 1 earns the swipe.** The hook is direct.
- **Slides 3-5 follow the same template.** Eyebrow + body structure repeats. Reader knows what to expect.
- **Slide 7 is the payoff.** The pause framing earns slide 8 (the CTA).
- **Slide 8 has one CTA.** lead-gen assessment link in bio.
- **Visual conventions hold.** Brand palette + fonts + spacing = recognizable Langley.

## What this carousel does NOT do

- No selfies or person photos (this is a framework carousel, not a personal post)
- No emoji
- No more than 4 lines on any slide
- No clickbait ("Wait til slide 7!")
- No "follow @jaredlangleycc for more" line

## Engagement targets

| Signal | Threshold | Diagnosis if under |
|---|---|---|
| Saves | 5% of impressions | The framework didn't earn save |
| Shares | 2% of impressions | The hook didn't earn share |
| Comments | 3+ in first 6 hours | The CTA or invite needs work |
| Profile visits | 5% of impressions | The visual didn't earn investigation |
| Link clicks (via bio link) | 1% of impressions | The CTA bridge is weak |

IG carousels live or die on save rate. Saves > likes for compounding reach.

## Production time

| Step | Time |
|---|---|
| Copy slides (using essay) | 15 min |
| Template fill in Figma / Canva | 15 min |
| Caption draft + voice scan | 10 min |
| Preview on phone | 5 min |
| Schedule via Meta Business Suite | 5 min |
| **Total** | **50 min** |

Once the user has a brand template, each carousel is ~50 minutes. Without a template, the first one takes 2 to 3 hours. Build the template first; amortize forever.

## When this carousel underperforms

Diagnose:

- **Slide 1 didn't earn the swipe.** Rework the hook. Test with a more arresting claim.
- **Slides 3-5 didn't earn the read.** Maybe the eyebrow / body / 3-line template is tired. Vary visual rhythm.
- **CTA slide didn't earn the click.** Maybe the offer isn't right for IG audience. Test newsletter signup instead of lead-gen assessment.
- **No saves.** The framework isn't worth saving. Either the framework is generic, or the visual didn't lock in the takeaway.

Test one variable at a time. Don't rework the whole carousel.
