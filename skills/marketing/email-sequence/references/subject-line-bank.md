# Subject Line Bank

50+ subject line patterns proven across welcome, nurture, win-back, and customer-onboarding sequences. Each one carries a principle. Use as a starting library, voice-scan before send.

## Welcome series subjects

### Email 1, Delivery + frame

- "Your [thing], as promised"
- "[Thing] inside"
- "Step 1 of 5"
- "Here's the [thing]. Quick note."
- "Open this first"
- "Welcome. Your [thing] is below."

Principle: state, don't sell. The subscriber knows why they're hearing from the user.

### Email 2, The story

- "I worked 60 hours and missed my number"
- "I caught myself doing the thing I tell clients not to"
- "The summer I built this"
- "Why I built [thing]"
- "Two years ago I was where you are"
- "Before this worked, this didn't"

Principle: specific moment, no abstraction.

### Email 3, The mistake

- "The thing most [audience] get wrong"
- "Three reactive triggers I see every week"
- "Stop doing this, it's costing you"
- "The mistake hiding in your [thing]"
- "What I almost taught you wrong"
- "If your [thing] feels [feeling], read this"

Principle: name the audience's actual pain.

### Email 4, The proof

- "How [name] went from [bad] to [good] in [time]"
- "What changed for [name]"
- "The shift that worked for [name]"
- "[Name]'s 90 days"
- "Three months ago [name] was stuck. Here's what moved."
- "[Name] tried this. Here's the result."

Principle: specific person, specific outcome.

### Email 5, The ask

- "Two weeks in. Asking now."
- "Quick offer."
- "If now is the time"
- "Direct ask"
- "Should we talk?"
- "[Offer], open through [date]"

Principle: plain. No urgency theater. No fake scarcity.

## Nurture / engagement subjects

- "Are you still in?"
- "Is this still useful?"
- "Quick question"
- "Still working on [problem]?"
- "Going through the list"
- "Checking in"
- "Three weeks later."
- "Was this helpful?"
- "One question for you"

Principle: human. Sounds like a friend.

## Win-back subjects

- "Going through the list this morning"
- "Saw your name"
- "Removing tomorrow"
- "Last email"
- "Cleaning the list"
- "If you want to stay"
- "One more before I clean the list"

Principle: clean, no guilt.

## Customer onboarding subjects

- "Step 1: [specific action]"
- "Welcome. Here's where to start."
- "The first thing"
- "Day 1 of 30"
- "Setup, 5 minutes"
- "The common pitfall (skip it)"
- "Quick win for week 1"
- "How are you doing?"
- "A check-in"
- "[Name] at day 30: [result]"
- "Renewal time"

Principle: action-oriented.

## Subjects to refuse (rewrite if user requests)

### Generic SaaS marketing tells

- "Unlock your potential"
- "Take your [thing] to the next level"
- "5 tips to [outcome]"
- "The ultimate guide to [thing]"
- "Don't miss this!"
- "Limited time"
- "Last chance"

### Caps + urgency theater

- "READ THIS NOW"
- "URGENT: [thing]"
- "Don't open if [condition]" (reverse psychology cliché)

### Preamble subjects

- "It's important to note"
- "Just checking in" (the word "just" is a hedge tell)
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "Quick favor"

### Personalization-token spam

- "[FirstName], your [thing] is here"
- "[FirstName], open this"
- "[FirstName], a question"

One personalization token per sequence is fine. More signals automation. Subscribers learn to scroll past.

## Subject-line principles cheat sheet

| Principle | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Specific over generic | "Three reactive triggers" | "5 tips for managers" |
| Direct over hyped | "Quick question" | "URGENT INSIDE!!!" |
| Plain over clever | "Removing tomorrow" | "Houston, we have a problem" |
| Curiosity over reveal | "What changed for Jordan" | "Here's why Jordan quadrupled her income" |
| Conversational over corporate | "Are you still in?" | "Subscriber engagement check-in" |
| Lowercase over title-case (mostly) | "going through the list" | "Going Through The List Today" |

## Preheader pairings

The preheader continues the subject. Together they tell the story.

| Subject | Preheader |
|---|---|
| "Your lead-gen assessment, as promised" | "Plus what to do with the result" |
| "I worked 60 hours and missed my number" | "And what changed the next quarter" |
| "Three reactive triggers I see every week" | "Pick one to kill before Friday" |
| "What changed for Jordan" | "Three months, one shift" |
| "Two weeks in. Asking now." | "If the moment is right, here's the next step" |
| "Going through the list this morning" | "Quick question, no pressure" |
| "Removing tomorrow" | "One click keeps you in" |

If the preheader is empty, the email client pulls the first line of the body. That's a leak. Always set the preheader.

## Length notes

- Subject: 5 to 9 words, 30 to 50 characters. Mobile clipping starts at ~30 characters on small screens.
- Preheader: 5 to 12 words, 50 to 80 characters.

Don't pack the subject. Let the preheader carry the rest.

## A/B test pairs (when at Stable Solo or above)

| Variant A | Variant B | Hypothesis tested |
|---|---|---|
| "Going through the list this morning" | "Quick question" | Specificity vs brevity |
| "I worked 60 hours and missed my number" | "The summer I built this" | Pain hook vs story hook |
| "Removing tomorrow" | "Last email" | Urgency framing |
| "Step 1: book your first session" | "Where to start" | Directive vs invitation |

Don't A/B test welcome email 1. Too few sends per cohort. Test on broadcasts and email 3 to 5 of the welcome series.

## Voice-rule overlay

Even after picking from this bank, run subject lines through the user's voice-rules:

- Em-dashes off by default → no em-dashes in subjects
- Banned phrases off → strip any matches
- Match archetype (operator subjects are sharper than storyteller subjects)

Show the subject line + voice check note before final lock.
