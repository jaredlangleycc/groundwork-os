# Sequence Shapes by Purpose

Beat-by-beat specs for each of the 4 lifecycle sequences. Use as a writing scaffold. Each beat is one paragraph or one section of the email, not the whole email.

## 1. Welcome series (post lead-magnet opt-in)

### Email 1, Deliver + frame

**Day 0. Send within 5 minutes of opt-in.**

Beat A: Confirm what they just got. ("Here's your [thing], as promised.")

Beat B: Set the expectation. ("Over the next 2 weeks I'll send 4 more emails. Each is short.")

Beat C: One reason to keep reading. ("Email 3 is the one most subscribers reply to.")

Beat D: First-name sign-off.

No P.S.

CTA: link to the lead magnet (in case the inline button fails). No call-booking yet.

Length: 80 to 150 words.

### Email 2, The story behind the claim

**Day 2.**

Beat A: A specific moment from the user's life that explains why they built this. ("I was running my first sales team. I'd just gotten promoted. I worked 60-hour weeks for 6 months and we still missed the quarter.")

Beat B: What changed. ("I stopped trying to do everything. I started doing one thing at a time, slowly. The quarter after that, we hit it.")

Beat C: The lesson, applied to the reader. ("If your week feels like the one I just described, you're not broken. You're miscoded.")

Beat D: First-name sign-off.

CTA: optional, link to a related blog post. NO offer pitch.

Length: 150 to 250 words.

### Email 3, The most common mistake

**Day 5.**

Beat A: Name the mistake. Specific. ("Most new sales managers think the problem is time management. It's not.")

Beat B: Explain what's actually happening. (1 to 2 paragraphs, teach one thing.)

Beat C: The shift. ("The fix isn't a calendar trick. It's a posture change.")

Beat D: Soft invitation. ("If this lands, reply with the moment in the last week that fits this pattern.")

Length: 200 to 300 words.

CTA: reply (not a link). Reply rate is the metric on this email.

### Email 4, The proof case

**Day 9.**

Beat A: One named example. (Anonymize if needed, but specific: "A client. B2B SaaS sales manager. 8 months in. Working 60-hour weeks.")

Beat B: What they did. (Specific actions, not abstract advice.)

Beat C: The result. ("Three months later: 45-hour weeks, hit number, sleeping again.")

Beat D: Optional connect-back to the reader. ("Your version of this is probably one decision you've been avoiding.")

Length: 200 to 300 words.

CTA: link to the offer page OR booking page, with a one-sentence framing. ("If you want to talk about your version of this, [calendar link].")

### Email 5, The direct ask

**Day 14.**

Beat A: Plain. ("Two weeks in. I haven't asked yet. Asking now.")

Beat B: The offer in one sentence. (What, who, how much, how long.)

Beat C: One sentence on whether the reader is the fit. ("If you're [situation], this fits.")

Beat D: One specific CTA.

Beat E: One sentence escape valve. ("If now isn't the moment, reply and tell me what's next for you. I read every one.")

Length: 150 to 250 words.

CTA: book a call OR buy. The same CTA the rest of the sequence has been quietly pointing at.

## 2. Nurture / engagement sequence (going cold)

### Email 1, Direct question

**Day 0.**

Beat A: Plain greeting. No "long time no see" theatrics.

Beat B: One question. ("Are you still working on [the problem you signed up for]?")

Beat C: One sentence on why you're asking. ("I clean the list every 60 days. If this isn't useful anymore, the unsubscribe link is at the bottom and I won't take it personally.")

Length: 80 to 120 words.

CTA: one link, the user's choice. ("Reply yes" OR "click here if so.")

### Email 2, The asset that re-engages

**Day 3.**

Send the best-performing piece you've ever written. The one with the most replies.

Beat A: Plain intro. ("This is the piece subscribers reply to most.")

Beat B: Link to the piece.

Beat C: Optional: one line on why it resonates.

Length: 60 to 100 words.

### Email 3, Final cut

**Day 7.**

Beat A: Plain. ("Cleaning the list tomorrow. If this is still useful, just reply with anything.")

Beat B: One sentence. ("If not, no action needed. I'll remove you.")

Length: 50 to 80 words.

After this email, if no engagement, remove from active list. Move to "Cold archive" group or unsubscribe.

## 3. Win-back sequence (cold 90+ days)

### Email 1, Personal note

**Day 0.**

Beat A: Plain greeting, first name.

Beat B: ("I'm going through the list this morning. Saw your name. You signed up for [thing] on [month, year].")

Beat C: One question. ("Still working on this?")

Beat D: One escape valve. ("Reply or click anywhere to stay. I'll assume silence is a no.")

Length: 80 to 120 words.

CTA: one link OR one reply ask.

### Email 2, Final

**Day 5.**

Beat A: Plain. ("Removing tomorrow.")

Beat B: One reason to stay. ("If you want to keep getting these, click here.")

Beat C: Optional P.S. with the smallest possible offer. (Soft, not aggressive.)

Length: 60 to 100 words.

After this, unsubscribe non-engagers.

## 4. Customer onboarding sequence (post-purchase)

### Email 1, Welcome + first action

**Day 0. Send within 1 hour of purchase.**

Beat A: Plain welcome. Thank.

Beat B: The one thing to do right now. (Not 5 things. One.)

Beat C: Where to get help if stuck.

Length: 100 to 150 words.

CTA: link to the first-action step.

### Email 2, Setup + common pitfall

**Day 1.**

Beat A: One pitfall most customers hit at this stage.

Beat B: How to avoid it.

Beat C: Soft check-in. ("How's setup going? Reply if stuck.")

Length: 150 to 200 words.

### Email 3, Quick win prompt

**Day 3.**

Beat A: The smallest visible result the customer can produce this week.

Beat B: How to produce it.

Beat C: An invitation to share the result (DM, reply, screenshot).

Length: 150 to 200 words.

### Email 4, Check in + question

**Day 7.**

One direct question. The user wants a reply.

Length: 80 to 120 words.

### Email 5, Case study

**Day 14.**

A named example of a customer who got the outcome at 30, 60, or 90 days. Sets the trajectory.

Length: 200 to 300 words.

### Email 6, Feedback ask

**Day 21.**

One question: "What's one thing you wish I'd done differently in the onboarding?"

Length: 60 to 100 words.

### Email 7, Renewal frame

**Day 30.**

Beat A: Recap what they've done in 30 days.

Beat B: What's possible in the next 30 to 90.

Beat C: The renewal or next-step offer.

Length: 200 to 300 words.

## Common cross-sequence rules

- Send from a real person's name + a real reply-to email.
- No "noreply@" addresses. Subscribers can reply, and replies are signal.
- Every email is one beat. If it has two beats, split it.
- Voice-scan every email before send (em-dashes, banned phrases, hedge words).
- Use the user's signature phrases naturally. Don't force them in every email.
- Track open + click + reply per email. Reply rate is the most important number on welcome series.
