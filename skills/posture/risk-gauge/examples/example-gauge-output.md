# Worked Example: Risk Gauge on the example coaching business

Two scenarios. The user is Jared, the example coaching business, Early Operator stage. Risk archetype: Low-Burn (free-first strict, max_tool_spend_monthly $50, max_client_hours_weekly 15, worst_case "missing rent or losing day-job income for more than one month").

## Scenario 1: Paid ads pitch

**Trigger.** Jared says: "I'm thinking about running $500/mo in LinkedIn Sponsored Content for three months to push the lead-gen assessment. The Coaching Accelerator program suggested it."

**Gauge output:**

```
RISK GAUGE
═══════════
THE MOVE: $500/mo LinkedIn Sponsored Content for 3 months ($1,500 total commit), driving to lead-gen assessment
THE BET: $1,500 cash, est. 4 hrs/wk to manage and review
PAYBACK: Need ~10 lead-gen assessment signups per $500 to break even, assuming 1 in 10 books a discovery call and 1 in 10 of those buys ($X,XXX)
  Math: $1,500 spend / $X,XXX deal = need 0.63 closes to break even, or ~63 high-quality leads at current funnel rates

ARCHETYPE CHECK: Low-Burn
VARIANCE FROM CAP: 10x over stated max_tool_spend_monthly ($50)
FREE PATH TRIED: Yes (organic LinkedIn, 3 months, ~30 posts, 5 meetings booked from 495 invites)

DOWNSIDE: $1,500 cash gone. Family floor possibly eaten by ad management. Day-job time pressure if monitoring slips to evenings.
WORST-CASE TOUCH: No (this is not rent-level money) but cumulative with other paid moves it gets there

FAMILY FLOOR: At risk
  4 hrs/wk of ad review + creative work needs to fit somewhere
  Day job + family floor + business = no slack
  This either replaces an existing $$ activity or eats family time
```

**The questions.**

1. The free path produced a 1% invite-to-book rate. What signal at week 2 of paid ads tells you it's working?
2. What signal at week 2 tells you to kill it?
3. The 4 hrs/wk of ad management has to come from somewhere. What gets dropped?
4. The Coaching Accelerator suggested this. Does their interest align with your interest? (They get paid for activity, you get paid for revenue.)
5. If LinkedIn produces zero lead-gen assessment signups in 30 days, are you killing it or doubling down? Decide now.

**Recommendation framing.** "This is over your cap. The free path produced signal. The question is not 'should I pay?' but 'is the paid version 10x faster than the free version at this audience size?' If yes, the bet is defensible at a smaller test ($300/mo for 30 days with a hard kill at no lead-gen assessment signups). If no, the answer is more free path, not paid path."

**Decision point.** Log via decision-logger either way.

## Scenario 2: Agency hire pitch

**Trigger.** Jared says: "I've been talking with a sales-enablement agency that wants $X,XXX/mo to handle outbound for me. They'll do the LinkedIn outreach, cold email, follow-ups, the whole thing. I keep the calls."

**Gauge output:**

```
RISK GAUGE
═══════════
THE MOVE: Hire a sales-enablement agency at $X,XXX/mo retainer to run all outbound (LI + cold email + follow-up)
THE BET: $X,XXX/mo cash, plus ~3 hrs/wk to brief the agency and review messaging, plus call time on calls they book
PAYBACK: 1 client/mo at $X,XXX = break-even on cash. But: opportunity cost of voice control and the relationship-building you do organically.

ARCHETYPE CHECK: Low-Burn
VARIANCE FROM CAP: 48x over stated max_tool_spend_monthly ($50)
FREE PATH TRIED: Yes (organic outbound, 3 months, 5 meetings from 495 invites)

DOWNSIDE: $X,XXX cash committed each month. Three-month minimum = $7,200 total. If they produce zero booked calls in the first 30 days, you're 1 month in on a 3-month contract.
WORST-CASE TOUCH: Yes
  $X,XXX/mo = ~1 month of household expenses at your level
  3-month commit = 3 months of household expense risk if zero return
  This is the rent-adjacent commit your worst_case named

FAMILY FLOOR: At risk
  Briefing time may slip to evenings (agencies want fast feedback)
  Voice mismatch between agency output and your voice will require constant correction
```

**The questions.**

1. Your worst-case is "missing rent for more than one month." This $X,XXX/mo over 3 months is 3 months of rent-equivalent. Are you ready to absorb that if zero leads convert?
2. The agency's voice will not be your voice. The brand built on "leadership for the whole person" delivered by an outbound team selling slots. Can you live with that?
3. Your free path produced 5 meetings from 495 invites. That's a known 1% rate. What rate does the agency need to hit to justify the spend?
4. Three-month minimum is the gauge's first flag. What's the exit clause? Can you kill at month 1 if it's not working?
5. Your day job still requires hours. The agency requires hours. The business requires hours. What gets dropped from the current week?

**Recommendation framing.** "This is far over cap and touches your worst-case. The math says you'd need to close 1 deal per month just to break even on cash, and that does not count the brand-voice risk or the management time. The 1% conversion rate is known. The agency's job is to prove they can do better. Ask for a 30-day pilot at $XXX/mo with a defined kill signal (e.g., 'zero qualified calls = no continuation'). If they refuse the pilot, the answer is no."

**Decision point.** This is a "not yet" recommendation. Log the move, the gauge output, and the user's call. If the user moves forward despite the gauge, log the override with their stated reason.

---

## What the gauge does not say

In neither scenario does the gauge say "do not do this." The gauge says "here is what you wrote down sober, here is the math, here is the family floor impact, here is the worst-case touch. The call is yours."

The user can override either gauge with eyes open. The skill exists to make sure the eyes are open.
