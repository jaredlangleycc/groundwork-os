THE 7 QUESTIONS (in order)

1. SET AGENDA: "What were you hoping to get out of our time today?"

2. CONTEXT: "Walk me through your week right now, hour by hour. What does
   a typical Tuesday look like?"

3. CONTEXT: "You wrote about missing the deals in your post on May 10.
   What's the specific moment you feel that most?"

4. PAIN-NAMING: "You also wrote 'I'm doing 30% of everything badly.' Stay
   with that for a second. Which 30%? And which thing feels worst?"

5. PAIN-NAMING: "When was the last time you took a Sunday completely off
   the laptop?" (If hedges: "Not a quiet Sunday. A Sunday where you
   didn't open Slack at all. When?")

6. OUTCOME: "If we had this conversation again in 90 days and it had
   gone really well, what would be different in your week? Be specific."

7. DECISION: "What's your process when you decide to invest in something
   like this? Is it just you, or is there a partner / boss involved?"

Q4 is the heart. The answer reveals what he's actually carrying.
Q5 tests the Wholeness hypothesis. If he can't remember the last
no-laptop Sunday, hypothesis is confirmed.

## Post-call notes (added retroactively, after the May 15 call happened)

What the brief got right:

- Q4 ("which 30%") got the real answer in 90 seconds: "I'm bad at 1:1s,
  I'm bad at forecasting, I'm bad at not jumping into rep calls."
- Q5 (Sunday question) confirmed hypothesis 1: "Honestly, last Sunday I
  reviewed 3 rep calls and built a forecast spreadsheet. My wife and I
  watched a movie. I was on my laptop the whole time."
- The Wholeness hypothesis was confirmed within 10 min.
- The redirect on his Q4 follow-up ("what do I do about it") worked
  exactly as scripted. He paused, reflected, and gave a deeper answer.

What the brief missed:

- Didn't predict he'd ask about format ("is this Zoom or Teams") which
  ate 3 minutes. Future briefs should note logistics questions briefly
  to acknowledge but not dwell.
- Didn't include a question about his daughter, which surfaced
  organically and was one of the most powerful moments. Future briefs
  for prospects who mention family on LinkedIn should include a direct
  family question.

Outcome:

- Alex closed to Close A (free intro call) within 5 min of close.
- Booked for Friday May 22, 3:00 PM PT.
- Pre-session form sent same day.
- HubSpot deal created, stage `clarity_booked`.

Time spent on brief: 22 minutes (target was 25). Brief was 600 words.
Used by Jared in the 10 min before the call. Felt prepped, not over-prepped.
