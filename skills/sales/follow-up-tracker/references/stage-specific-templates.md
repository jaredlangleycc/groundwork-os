# Stage-Specific Follow-up Templates

25+ proven follow-up templates organized by stage and stuck-pattern. All voice-scanned for the common antipatterns (em dashes, "X, not Y", hedge words, AI tells, dead closes).

Use these as scaffolds. Personalize the first line. Strip what doesn't fit.

## Lead stage

### L1: No response to first cold email (touch 2 of sequence already covered, this is post-sequence)

> "{{first_name}}, two weeks back I sent a note about [topic]. Didn't hear back, no worries. Quick check: is [the topic] still on your radar this quarter, or is it filed under "maybe Q3"? Send me one of those two answers and I'll know how to plan the next conversation."

### L2: Soft "not now" reply

> "{{first_name}}, you mentioned [their reason: focus on Q1 / hiring / runway / other priority] when I reached out. Standard check-in: how's that going? If you're still heads-down, all good. If the dust is settling, the door is open on the [topic] conversation."

### L3: Mutual intro went cold

> "{{first_name}}, [mutual] introduced us back on [date]. I followed up but the thread went quiet. If timing is off, send a sentence and I'll back off. If the intro made sense and we just lost the thread, here's my calendar: [link]."

### L4: Webinar / event attendee no reply

> "{{first_name}}, you attended [event] in [month]. I sent the resource I mentioned during Q&A. Did it land? If you read it and have thoughts, hit reply. If it never showed up, send a one-word "resend" and I'll forward."

### L5: They downloaded something, never engaged

> "{{first_name}}, you grabbed [resource] back in [month]. I don't track engagement post-download, but I do follow up. Did the resource land, miss, or get lost in tabs? One-word answer and I'll plan the next thing."

## Discovery Booked stage

### DB1: 24-hour confirmation (default)

> "{{first_name}}, confirming tomorrow at [time] [timezone]. Calendar link if anything moved: [link]. Quick context: I'll come with 2 questions on [topic 1] and [topic 2]. Anything you'd like me to come prepped on, send a sentence."

### DB2: Same-day rescheduler (call moved 3+ times)

> "{{first_name}}, I want this conversation to happen but the calendar keeps shifting. If now isn't the right window, no pressure. Send me a 2-week range that's likely to hold and I'll lock something in then."

### DB3: No-show recovery (day-of, no show)

> "{{first_name}}, we had [time] today on the calendar. I held the slot. Things come up, that's life. If you still want the conversation, here's the rebook link: [link]. If something changed and the call isn't relevant now, send a sentence and I'll close it out."

### DB4: Pre-call value drop (24 hours out, prospect is senior)

> "{{first_name}}, confirming tomorrow at [time]. Wanted to share one piece of context ahead of time: [link to relevant 1-pager or resource]. No need to read in advance. It's just so the conversation can start from a deeper baseline."

## Discovery Held stage

### DH1: User owes proposal (default after good call)

> "{{first_name}}, great conversation on [date]. I owe you the [proposal / scope / one-pager]. Sending it [specific date]. One last thing to make sure I aim it right: [one sharp question you should have asked on the call, that will sharpen the proposal]. Reply with a sentence and I'll have the doc over."

### DH2: User owes resource / case study

> "{{first_name}}, you mentioned [topic] on our call. The case study I referenced is here: [link]. The part most relevant to your situation is on page [X]. After you read it, reply with one question or one reaction and we can take the next step."

### DH3: Prospect owes intro / information

> "{{first_name}}, on our call you mentioned you'd loop in [name / role] for the next conversation. Quick check, has that happened, or has it slipped down the list? If it slipped, no judgment, that's normal. Send me a sentence on the new timing and I'll plan around it."

### DH4: Vague-call recovery (call went sideways, no clear next step set)

> "{{first_name}}, our conversation on [date] left me thinking. Here's what I'm hearing as the actual opportunity, in your own words: '[paraphrase their words back, 1-2 sentences].' If that's right, the next step is [specific 30-min thing]. If I'm hearing it wrong, send me the correction."

### DH5: Multi-stakeholder stall (they need to convince a partner / boss)

> "{{first_name}}, you mentioned needing to bring [other person] into the conversation. Two options: A) I can join the next conversation with them, send me dates. B) I can send you a 1-pager built for [their role] that you can forward. Which is more useful right now?"

## Proposal Sent stage

### PS1: A/B/C close (default after 7 days)

> "{{first_name}}, sent the proposal on [date]. Three things could be true: A) it landed and you're internalizing. B) something on the proposal is off and worth a conversation. C) it's a no for now. Send me one letter and I'll respond accordingly."

### PS2: Decision-window close (after 14 days)

> "{{first_name}}, the proposal has been out for 2 weeks. I want to respect your timeline. Two paths: I can hold the spot for another week while you decide, or I can release it and we'll revisit when timing is better. Which fits?"

### PS3: Champion ask (they want it, internal blocker)

> "{{first_name}}, sounds like you're a yes and the holdup is on [budget / approval / partner]. Anything I can send to help that internal conversation? Happy to do a 15-min Q&A with [decision-maker], or send a tailored 1-pager built for [their concern]."

### PS4: Scope renegotiation (they pushed back on price)

> "{{first_name}}, your push on price is fair, and I'd rather adjust the scope than drop the rate. Two scope options I can build at a lower price: A) [smaller scope, lower price]. B) [phased approach, same total over longer window]. Want me to draft either as a new proposal?"

### PS5: The walk (proposal stalled past 21 days, user needs to release)

> "{{first_name}}, I'm closing the loop on the proposal from [date]. The slot I held for you is going to another prospect this week. If something changed and you want to reopen, send a sentence and I'll restart. If not, all good, the door stays open for future."

## Verbal Yes stage

### VY1: Contract / invoice nudge (default, 3 days after verbal)

> "{{first_name}}, looping back on the verbal from [date]. To get started, the next step is [signing the contract / paying the deposit / etc.]. The [link / attachment] is here: [link]. If anything changed on your end since we talked, send a sentence and I'll adjust."

### VY2: Pre-kickoff confirmation (7 days after verbal, no payment yet)

> "{{first_name}}, kickoff is scheduled for [date], assuming we get [contract signed / invoice paid] this week. If timing slipped and we need to move kickoff, send me your new window. If you're stuck on [the blocker], let me know what to push on."

### VY3: Soft close-loop (14 days post-verbal, ghosted)

> "{{first_name}}, the verbal from [date] hasn't translated to [signed contract / payment]. That happens for real reasons. Quick close-loop: are we still on, are we delayed, or has the situation changed? Any of the three is fine, I just need to know to plan."

## "On You" reminders (when user owes the prospect)

These are NOT sent to the prospect. They're internal reminders surfaced in the weekly sweep.

### OY1: User owes proposal (default)
- Action: write the proposal this week
- Suggested skill handoff: `/proposal-writer`
- Time block: 90-120 min

### OY2: User owes case study / resource
- Action: dig out the asset, send with 1-line context
- Time block: 15 min per prospect

### OY3: User owes intro (to peer / vendor / advisor)
- Action: write the intro email, send (if intro is mutual)
- Time block: 10 min per prospect

### OY4: User owes scoping doc
- Action: write 1-page scope, send
- Time block: 45-60 min

### OY5: User owes follow-up to discovery question
- Action: answer the question they asked on the call, send
- Time block: 10-15 min per prospect

## Banned phrases (the voice-rules skill enforces, listed here for reference)

Words and phrases to NEVER appear in any follow-up:

- "Circling back"
- "Just checking in"
- "Just wanted to bump this"
- "Following up on my last email"
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I hope you're doing well"
- "Just making sure you saw"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "Touching base"
- "Per my last email"
- "As discussed"
- "Looking forward to hearing from you"
- "Hope to connect soon"
- "Sorry to bother you"
- "Quick question" (as subject line)

Replacements:
- For "circling back" → state the new context that justifies the message
- For "just checking in" → ask a specific question
- For "looking forward to" → state the next concrete step or end the email
