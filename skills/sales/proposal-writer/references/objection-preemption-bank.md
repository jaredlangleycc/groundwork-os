# Objection Preemption Bank

20+ proactive FAQ items the proposal can include to address objections before they kill the deal. Pick the 4-6 most relevant to the specific prospect, based on what they raised during discovery.

Organized by objection type. Each item includes the framing question, the actual FAQ text, and notes on when to include.

## Price objections

### O1: "Is the investment worth it"

Question: "How do I know this is worth the investment?"

FAQ text:
> "Most clients see specific shifts by week 4-6, and the engagement pays for itself by week 12 in terms of revenue, time, or both. If by day 30 you've decided this isn't the right fit, full refund. The risk is on me to deliver, not on you to gamble."

Include when: Prospect mentioned cost or asked about ROI during discovery.

### O2: "Why this price and not less"

Question: "Why $X and not a lower price?"

FAQ text:
> "The price reflects the depth of the engagement: 12 weekly sessions, async access, custom roadmap. A lower-priced engagement would have less time or less customization. Both versions exist in the market. The right question is whether this depth is what your situation needs."

Include when: Prospect compared price to a competitor or asked for a discount.

### O3: "Can I pay monthly"

Question: "Do you offer a payment plan?"

FAQ text:
> "Yes. Two options: pay in full at $X (one payment), or three monthly payments of $Y each. Both are charged at the same total. Monthly is recommended when cash flow needs spreading out. Pay-in-full simplifies bookkeeping."

Include when: Prospect mentioned budget timing or cash flow.

## Timing objections

### O4: "Right time to start"

Question: "When's the right time to start this?"

FAQ text:
> "Most clients tell me the 'right time' would have been 6 months ago. The work happens regardless of timing if it's the right work. Practical answer: kickoff session within 7 days of signing, sessions adjust to your calendar. If you have a known scheduling block in the next 90 days (vacation, board prep, etc.), name it on the kickoff call and we plan around it."

Include when: Prospect hedged on timing or mentioned a busy season.

### O5: "What if I'm too busy to do the work"

Question: "What if I can't do the homework between sessions?"

FAQ text:
> "The engagement assumes 60-90 min per week beyond the sessions: reflection, application, the one experiment we agree on. If a week gets crushed, we adjust the next session to start where you are. The goal isn't perfect compliance with a curriculum, it's specific shifts that hold up under your real schedule."

Include when: Prospect named workload concerns.

### O6: "Can we pause if life happens"

Question: "What if I need to pause mid-engagement?"

FAQ text:
> "Built in. Up to 4 weeks of pause across the 90-day engagement, no cost adjustment. Beyond 4 weeks of pause, we re-evaluate together. Life happens. The engagement is supposed to flex, not break."

Include when: Prospect has known life-event coming up.

## Fit / scope objections

### O7: "Have you worked with someone like me"

Question: "Have you worked with [my type of role/company/stage]?"

FAQ text:
> "Yes, with [N] clients in [closest match category]. The reason the work translates: [1-sentence reason]. The reason your situation is unique: [acknowledge the difference]. The first 30 days we test fit. If by day 30 the engagement isn't aimed at your specific situation, refund."

Include when: Prospect questioned domain fit or compared to other client types.

### O8: "What if the engagement isn't right for me"

Question: "What if I sign up and it turns out not to fit?"

FAQ text:
> "30-day full refund, no questions asked. You decide by day 30 whether to continue or pull back. After day 30, the engagement runs to day 90 with no further refund window. The math: you risk 30 days of effort to know if this works. I risk the engagement fee + delivery time to earn the rest of the engagement."

Include when: Prospect hesitated on commitment.

### O9: "What's NOT included"

Question: "What does the engagement NOT cover?"

FAQ text:
> "The engagement does not include: team coaching (priced separately at $X), 24/7 emergency access, content production or deliverable rework outside scope. If something outside scope comes up during the engagement, we name it and decide together whether to add it (at additional cost) or defer it."

Include when: Always include if scope is dense. Helpful preemption.

## Decision-process objections

### O10: "Need to convince partner / boss"

Question: "I need to run this by [partner / boss / co-founder]. How should I frame it?"

FAQ text:
> "Three options:
> A) I can join a 30-min call with [decision-partner] to answer their questions directly. No cost.
> B) I can send a 1-pager built for [their role] that you can forward.
> C) You forward this proposal as-is.
> Option A is highest-converting. Option B is fastest. Tell me which fits."

Include when: Prospect mentioned a partner or boss in discovery.

### O11: "Need to think about it"

Question: "How long should I take to decide?"

FAQ text:
> "The proposal has a 14-day decision deadline (see cover). Two weeks is enough time to internalize, talk to whoever needs to weigh in, and decide. If you need more time, send me a sentence and I'll extend. If you decide earlier, send a sentence and we kick off sooner."

Include when: Always include. The deadline is built in.

### O12: "What if I want to extend"

Question: "If the engagement is working, can I extend?"

FAQ text:
> "Yes. Extensions are negotiated at the mid-engagement review (week 6). Two formats: a) Ongoing monthly continuation at $X/mo. b) Another 90-day sprint at $X. Extension is optional. Most clients take 30-60 days after the engagement to integrate before deciding."

Include when: Prospect signaled long-term interest.

## Practical objections

### O13: "How do sessions work"

Question: "What does a session actually look like?"

FAQ text:
> "60 min on Microsoft Teams or Zoom (your preference). Standard structure: 10-min check-in on what shifted since last session, 35-min on the core work for this week, 15-min on what to take away + commit to. Sessions are recorded for your reference. You can rewatch anytime."

Include when: Prospect asked about logistics or seemed unfamiliar with coaching format.

### O14: "How do I cancel a session"

Question: "What's the cancellation policy?"

FAQ text:
> "24-hour notice to reschedule, no penalty. Less than 24 hours, the session is considered held. Exception: real emergencies, no penalty regardless of notice. Sessions are scheduled in 90-day blocks at kickoff, with flexibility to shift within the week."

Include when: Prospect has unpredictable schedule.

### O15: "What happens after the 90 days"

Question: "What happens after the engagement ends?"

FAQ text:
> "Three paths:
> A) You take what you've built and run with it. No further commitment. Door open if you want to come back later.
> B) Continue ongoing at $X/mo, lighter cadence (1-2 sessions/mo).
> C) Another 90-day sprint at $X with a new focus.
> Most clients pick A or B. The right choice depends on what you've integrated by day 90."

Include when: Prospect asked about long-term relationship.

## Risk / refund objections

### O16: "How does the refund work"

Question: "Walk me through the 30-day refund."

FAQ text:
> "From day 1 to day 30, you can ask for a full refund for any reason. Process: reply to my email saying you want to invoke the refund. I send the refund within 5 business days. No exit interview required, no judgment, no haggling. After day 30, the engagement runs to day 90 with no further refund window."

Include when: Prospect hesitated on commitment or asked about guarantees.

### O17: "What if it doesn't work"

Question: "What if I do the engagement and don't see results?"

FAQ text:
> "Two answers:
> Short answer: Use the 30-day refund window. If by day 30 the engagement isn't aimed at your real situation, refund.
> Long answer: 'Doesn't work' usually means one of two things. Either the engagement isn't right (use refund). Or the work isn't happening between sessions (which is different, and we'd address it directly). I name which one I see by week 4, you decide what to do with that information."

Include when: Prospect explicitly asked about failure case.

### O18: "Is there a contract"

Question: "Do I need to sign a contract?"

FAQ text:
> "Yes. A short engagement agreement (2 pages) covers: scope, payment, confidentiality, IP, the refund clause, cancellation. No 50-page boilerplate. Sent for e-signature after you indicate yes. Standard turnaround is 24-48 hours."

Include when: Always include for first-time engagements.

## Brand / credibility objections

### O19: "Why you and not [competitor]"

Question: "Why work with you specifically?"

FAQ text:
> "Honest answer: the work I do is shaped around [user's distinct framework, e.g., the Three Pillars]. If that framework resonates with how you think about leadership, the work translates. If a more transactional, tactical coaching fit is what you need, I can refer you to someone better positioned for that. Decision: does [framework] match your situation."

Include when: Prospect compared you to competitors.

### O20: "How long have you been doing this"

Question: "What's your background?"

FAQ text:
> "[User's credential statement, 2-3 sentences from CLAUDE.md]. The shorter version: [user's strongest one-line credibility]. More detail on the About page and LinkedIn if useful."

Include when: Prospect asked about background or seemed to need more reassurance.

## How to choose which FAQs to include

A proposal has 5-7 FAQ slots. Selection rules:

1. Always include: O11 (decision timeline), O16 (refund), O18 (contract)
2. Match to discovery: Pick 2-3 that surface what THIS prospect asked about
3. Bonus: O7 (have you worked with someone like me) if prospect questioned fit

Don't include all 20. The FAQ should feel customized, not comprehensive. Pick the right 5-7.
