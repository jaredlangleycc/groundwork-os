# Positioning: the example coaching business

v1.0
Locked: 2026-05-18
Source: icp-discovery v1.0 + 8 customer interviews + 12 prospect data points from Accelerator funnel

## The statement

I help frontline sales managers in their first 18 months at B2B SaaS companies (50-500 employees) hit their team quota and their own number through a 90-day coaching engagement with weekly 1:1s, unlike traditional executive coaching that runs 12+ months at C-suite price points and treats family and career as separate problems.

---

## Parts (for reference)

- **ICP:** frontline sales managers in their first 18 months at B2B SaaS companies (50-500 employees)
- **Outcome:** hit their team quota and their own number
- **Approach:** a 90-day coaching engagement with weekly 1:1s
- **Alternative:** traditional executive coaching
- **Shortcoming:** runs 12+ months at C-suite price points and treats family and career as separate problems

---

## Self-selection test

**Right buyer reaction (predicted, validated by 8 interviews):**
"That is exactly me. I am a new sales manager, I am at a 200-person SaaS company, I am missing quota, and I do not have a year or $50K to give to an executive coach. And the 'family and career as separate problems' line is the thing nobody else says out loud."

**Wrong buyer reaction (predicted, validated by 4 declined prospects):**
"Not for me. I am a VP of Sales with 8 years of leadership experience" or "Not for me. I am a solopreneur with no team" or "Not for me. I want quarterly executive coaching at a $50K price band."

The self-selection is operationally working. Discovery calls in May 2026 show qualified buyers self-select in within the first 5 minutes. Unqualified buyers self-select out in the booking form.

## Update triggers (when to re-run positioning)

Re-run this skill when:

1. **ICP changes.** If `icp-discovery` shifts the ICP (e.g., adds a sub-segment or pivots role), positioning must update.
2. **Offer changes substantially.** If `offer-design` adds a new tier or changes the structure (e.g., adds a group cohort variant), positioning must update.
3. **Pricing tier moves.** If price goes from $X,XXX to $4,800, the alternative comparison in Part 5 may shift (compared category changes).
4. **Competitor lands similar positioning.** If a new entrant claims similar territory, the differentiator narrows. Re-tighten.
5. **Close rate drops without clear cause.** If positioning was performing and stops, the market may have moved. Re-run customer interviews and re-position.
6. **Quarterly review.** Default cadence. Even without trigger, validate the positioning is still operational every 90 days.

## Notes on what works in this positioning

### "Family and career as separate problems" is the operational differentiator

In 5 of 8 customer interviews, the buyer mentioned wanting work that "did not eat my family time." This is not a coaching-industry-standard phrase. Executive coaching does not say this. Sales training does not say this. The Langley positioning owns this phrase because the founder built the offer around it (Foundation framework's "Wholeness" pillar).

### Naming the alternative + shortcoming honestly

Some coaches refuse to name executive coaching as the alternative because they want to leave the door open to selling executive-level engagements later. Langley's positioning closes that door deliberately. The trade-off: the positioning sacrifices breadth at higher price points to gain depth at the $X,XXX band where the volume actually exists.

### The 90-day commitment makes the price legible

"$X,XXX over 90 days" is operationally specific. The buyer can map it to their forecasting horizon. Executive coaching engagements that quote "$50K starting" without scope feel unlimited and scary. The 90-day frame makes the spend feel bounded and survivable.

### What the positioning is NOT good at

- It does not work for first-time managers OUTSIDE B2B SaaS (e.g., field sales, retail, professional services). Those buyers do not recognize themselves in the ICP language.
- It does not work for second-time managers (people 2-5 years into leadership). They want peer-level coaching, not foundational coaching.
- It does not work in C-suite. The price band signals "junior" and the buying frame is wrong.

These limitations are by design. Re-positioning to capture those segments would require sacrificing the sharpness in the current ICP.
