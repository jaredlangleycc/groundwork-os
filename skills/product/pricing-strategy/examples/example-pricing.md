## The 5 inputs (decision-time analysis, 2026-04-26)

### Input 1: Willingness to pay (from icp-discovery)

From 8 customer interviews + 4 declined-prospect notes:

- 5 of 8 interviewees mentioned a budget range of $2,000-$5,000 for "coaching that actually works"
- 2 of 8 mentioned they would pay $7,000-$10,000 "if I were sure it would deliver"
- 1 of 8 said budget would need to be under $1,500 (likely junior-trap buyer; not ideal ICP)
- 4 declined prospects: 2 declined for price reasons; 2 declined for fit reasons

**WTP band:** $2,000 to $5,000, with premium ceiling at $7-10K for high-confidence buyers.

### Input 2: Unit economics

- **Delivery hours per engagement:** 12 calls × 60 min + 30 min prep × 12 + 6 hrs admin/email over 90 days = ~26 hours
- **Direct cost per engagement:** ~$50 (Zoom, Notion, document storage)
- **Capacity ceiling:** 4-5 active engagements at any time (at 12-15 hrs/wk in the business)

At $X,XXX:
- $2,350 net per engagement (price minus direct cost)
- $92/hr blended (across all delivery hours)
- 4 active × $X,XXX / 90 days = ~$10.6K/mo MRR equivalent
- Margin: very high (~98% gross)

At $3,200 (potential increase):
- $122/hr blended
- 4 active × $3,200 / 90 days = ~$14.2K/mo MRR equivalent
- Same margin profile

The math works at both price points if close rate stays above 30%.

### Input 3: Comparable market pricing (researched 2026-04-26)

**Direct competitors (solo sales-leader coaches):**

- [Competitor A]: $1,500 for 60-day engagement (junior trap; cheap signaling)
- [Competitor B]: $4,200 for 6-month engagement (operational band, longer duration)
- [Competitor C]: $3,500 for 90 days, no money-back guarantee
- [Competitor D]: $X,XXX for 6-month "executive sales leadership" program

**Indirect competitors (executive coaching firms):**

- Mid-tier executive coaching (BetterUp, Coach.me Pro): $200-400/hr × ~12 sessions = $X,XXX-4,800
- Senior executive coaching (independent practitioners): $300-600/hr × ~12 sessions = $3,600-7,200
- Tier-1 executive coaching (Marshall Goldsmith Stakeholder Centered, similar): $25,000-100,000 per engagement

**Substitute solutions:**

- Self-paced sales-management courses (Sales Hacker, Pavilion CMM): $300-1,500
- Internal company training programs: included in employment (no cash cost to buyer)
- Doing nothing: $0 (and visible cost in missed quota, role at risk)

**Anchor map summary:**

- Junior trap: $500-1,500 (e.g., Competitor A)
- Lower operational: $1,500-3,500 (Langley sits here at $X,XXX)
- Mid operational: $3,500-7,000 (could move here with stronger positioning)
- Premium: $7,000-15,000 (executive coaching territory)
- Elite: $25,000+ (out of band for solo without tier-1 brand)

### Input 4: Risk profile

From `risk-gauge` (Langley assessment):

- Stage: Early Operator
- Risk profile: Calculated
- Stated revenue goal: $10,000/mo by Q3 2026
- Family floor: M-F, 4-7pm protected for family time, 9am-3pm PT for business

**Risk implication:** raising prices is fine as a calculated test (not aggressive). A 33% increase ($X,XXX → $3,200) is at the upper bound of "calculated."

### Input 5: Stated revenue goal

- **90-day goal (Q2 2026):** $10K MRR
- **12-month goal:** $25K MRR with a second offer (cohort or higher-tier 1:1)

Current pricing math:
- $X,XXX × 4 active = $9,600/mo equivalent (close to $10K goal)
- Pipeline must produce 1.3 closed engagements per month to maintain 4 active

At $3,200:
- $3,200 × 3 active = $9,600/mo (slightly under)
- $3,200 × 4 active = $12,800/mo (above $10K goal, with headroom)

Conclusion: $3,200 is the right test price IF close rate stays at or above 30%.

## Decision rationale (locked at $X,XXX, not $3,200)

After running the 5 inputs, the recommendation was to TEST $3,200 with the next 5 prospects. The decision was to HOLD at $X,XXX for the following reasons:

1. **Validation gap:** Only 5 closed engagements at $X,XXX. Need 5-8 more to lock the price-close-rate baseline before testing an increase.

2. **Brand asset gap:** At $3,200+, buyers expect more visible signals (testimonials, case studies, thought leadership). Langley has 3 testimonials (Sam, Jordan, anonymized Riley), which is appropriate for $X,XXX but thin for $3,200+.

3. **Outcome data gap:** No documented outcome data yet (flagship engagement is too new for 90-day-post outcome reporting). Outcome data is what supports premium pricing.

4. **Revenue goal achievable at $X,XXX:** $9,600/mo from 4 active engagements is within striking distance of the $10K MRR goal. No financial pressure forcing a price increase.

5. **Sales motion not validated:** Cold email channel just launching in June. Need to see close rate per channel at $X,XXX before testing higher.

**Decision:** Hold $X,XXX through Q2 2026. Next review at 2026-Q3 quarterly check-in. If outcome data + testimonials + close rate are all healthy, test $3,200 with 5 prospects in Q3.

## Money-back guarantee rationale

The 30-day money-back guarantee was added at v0.5.0 of the offer (2026-03-28). Decision rationale:

**Pros of guarantee:**

- Closes the price objection ("what if it does not work?")
- Forces the seller to deliver fast value in first 30 days
- Lifts close rate by an estimated 20-30% (based on cross-domain data; not Langley-specific)

**Cons (mitigated):**

- Refund risk: mitigated by ICP-fit screening on discovery call (only confirmed-fit buyers progress to proposal)
- Coaching delivery in first 30 days: structured to produce visible value (Foundation document outlined by week 4)

**Actual refund history (as of 2026-05-23):**

- 0 refunds in 5 closed engagements
- 0 refund requests
- 1 buyer in active engagement (Alex, day 1 as of 5/23)

The guarantee is operationally working as designed. No reason to remove.

## Tier design (not pursued)

Langley considered tiered pricing (Foundation Lite at $1,200, Foundation Standard at $X,XXX, Foundation Premium at $4,800) at v0.5.0 of the offer. Rejected for these reasons:

1. **Single offer not yet validated at scale.** Building 3 tiers before validating 1 is premature.
2. **No clear differentiation between tiers** without arbitrary scope additions (more calls, more access). This is the "Bronze/Silver/Gold trap."
3. **Operational complexity.** 3 tiers means 3 sales pages, 3 proposals, 3 delivery workflows. Capacity better spent on landing more $X,XXX buyers.
4. **The Ongoing Coaching offer is the real second tier.** Post-Foundation, the $250/$350 monthly retainer serves the buyer who wants extended access. This is a sensible two-offer ladder.

Re-evaluate tier design in 2026-Q3 if:
- 10+ engagements closed at $X,XXX
- A pattern emerges of buyers asking for either lower-priced entry OR higher-priced premium
- The seller has bandwidth for a second offer (cohort, group, productized variant)

## Pricing applied to channels

Different channels have different close-rate-at-price patterns:

### Inbound (lead-gen assessment + Substack/blog)

- Volume: ~3-5 qualified leads per month (May 2026)
- Close rate at $X,XXX: ~40% (inbound buyers self-select strongly)
- No discount; pay $X,XXX

### Cold outreach (LinkedIn + cold email, launching June 2026)

- Volume target: 2-3 booked discovery calls per month
- Close rate projection: 20-30%
- Same price: $X,XXX

If cold-outreach close rate drops below 15%, consider a "cold-outreach-specific" promo (e.g., "for the first 3 cold-outreach engagements per quarter, $1,800 to gather case studies"). This would be a one-time test, not a permanent discount.

### Referral

- Volume: 1-2 per month (rare but high-quality)
- Close rate: 60-80%
- Same price: $X,XXX

Referrals are the highest-converting channel. No discount; the trust built by the referrer carries the price.

## What this pricing is NOT optimized for

- **Maximum lifetime value.** Foundation is fixed-engagement; lifetime value depends on Ongoing Coaching uptake (which is currently low-volume).
- **Maximum unit margin.** Could price at $3,200 or higher with stronger positioning. Current price is conservative.
- **Premium positioning.** $X,XXX anchors mid-operational, not premium. Premium positioning requires stronger brand assets.

The current price is optimized for: SOLVENT EARLY OPERATOR CAPACITY. The seller needs 4 active engagements to hit the $10K MRR goal, the buyer needs to feel safe to commit, and the engagement needs to be sustainable at the seller's hours-per-week.

The price will move up as the brand, the testimonials, the outcome data, and the close-rate-at-price-point evidence accumulate.
