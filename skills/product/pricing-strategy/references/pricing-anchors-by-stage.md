# Pricing Anchors by Stage

Reference table for typical pricing bands across common business models at each NightShiftOS stage. Use as a starting reference, not a target. Actual pricing depends on the user's willingness-to-pay research, unit economics, positioning, and risk profile.

The "junior trap" band is where most first-time founders price (because they price from fear). The "premium ceiling" band is what 90% of solo founders never reach (because they fail to anchor positioning + reputation + outcomes). The "operational band" is where most healthy solo businesses live.

---

## Model 1: Solo 1:1 coaching engagement (90-day fixed)

### Junior trap

$500 - $1,500 total

Buyers in this band:
- Friends and family
- People who cannot afford coaching but want it
- People testing a coach with low commitment

Sellers in this band:
- First-time coaches without testimonials
- Sellers pricing from imposter syndrome
- Sellers without positioning sharpness

Why this band traps:
- Cannot sustain margin at solo capacity (you need 8-10 active clients to make any meaningful income, which is too many for solo delivery)
- Signals "junior" to buyers who would pay more
- Self-selects buyers who do not engage in the work (low-priced engagements have lower completion rates)

### Operational band

$1,500 - $5,000 total

Buyers in this band:
- Personal credit card spend (under $3,000 threshold for most)
- Mid-career professionals investing in themselves
- Sales managers, founders pre-Series A, solo professionals

Sellers in this band:
- First 1-2 years of solo coaching
- Pre-Rev through Stable Solo
- Coaches with 3-10 testimonials

Why this band works:
- Capacity math: 4-5 active clients × $2-4K each = $8-20K MRR, achievable solo
- Buyer math: $2-4K over 90 days is "discretionary" for most ICP profiles
- Margin: very high (low overhead)

### Premium ceiling

$5,000 - $15,000 total

Buyers in this band:
- Company-paid expenses (CEO discretion, training budget)
- Mid-stage founders with revenue
- Sales VPs and Directors investing in themselves out of bonus

Sellers in this band:
- 3-5+ years of solo coaching
- Documented outcome data (specific client wins)
- Strong positioning + brand assets
- Stable Solo or Scaling Solo

Why this ceiling is rare for solo:
- Most solo coaches do not invest in the brand assets, positioning, and reputation that justify premium pricing
- Buyers at this price band expect more than 1:1 calls (group access, content library, faster response times)

### Above $15K (executive coaching territory)

Buyers in this band:
- Company-paid C-suite engagements
- Founders of funded startups
- VPs and SVPs with executive development budgets

Sellers in this band:
- Coaches with 10+ years experience
- Coaches with published thought leadership (books, keynotes)
- Coaches with named C-suite client roster
- Specialist niches (executive transition coaching, board readiness)

Most NightShiftOS users will not operate at this band. Skip unless positioning specifically targets it.

## Model 2: Group cohort / mastermind (8-12 weeks)

### Junior trap

$100 - $497 per seat

- Comparable to courses, not cohort
- Cohort dynamic does not justify the price differential vs. self-paced
- Operational complexity (running a live cohort) does not pencil out

### Operational band

$500 - $2,500 per seat

- Sustainable for 15-25 person cohorts
- Founder runs 2-4 cohorts per year
- Revenue per cohort: $15-50K

### Premium ceiling

$2,500 - $7,500 per seat

- High-context cohorts (executive-level, deeply specialized)
- 8-15 person cohorts (smaller is more intimate, justifies higher price)
- Includes some 1:1 component (e.g., quarterly check-ins)

### Above $7,500

- Elite cohorts (e.g., Pavilion CMM, On Deck Founders)
- Brand-name accelerators
- Multi-year commitments

## Model 3: Productized service (one-time fixed-scope deliverable)

### Junior trap

$500 - $2,500

- Most one-time deliverables (audit, brand kit, website) take 20-40 hours to do well
- Below $2,500 = hourly rate under $100/hr blended (low for skilled work)

### Operational band

$2,500 - $15,000

- Website redesigns ($5-15K)
- Brand identity packages ($5-15K)
- Marketing audits ($2.5-7.5K)
- Technical assessments ($5-12K)

### Premium ceiling

$15,000 - $50,000

- Custom enterprise-grade deliverables
- Complex multi-stakeholder projects
- Niche specialists (legal, security, accessibility audits)

### Above $50,000

- Custom development projects
- Multi-month consulting engagements
- Enterprise procurement-mediated work

## Model 4: Retainer / fractional engagement (monthly recurring)

### Junior trap

$500 - $2,500/mo

- Hourly rate below $75-100/hr blended
- Cannot sustain at any senior level

### Operational band

$3,000 - $10,000/mo

- Fractional roles (CMO, CFO, CTO at small companies)
- 10-20 hours/wk of seller time
- 6-month minimum commitment standard

### Premium ceiling

$10,000 - $25,000/mo

- Senior fractional roles at funded startups
- 20-40 hours/wk seller time
- Often includes equity or board seat component

### Above $25,000/mo

- Full-time equivalent fractional work
- Outsourced executive offices (CFO firm style)
- Niche expertise at funded companies

## Model 5: Course (self-paced, evergreen)

### Junior trap

$0 - $97

- Free or near-free
- Used for lead generation, not revenue
- Lifetime value depends on upsell to coaching or cohort

### Operational band

$97 - $997

- Standard one-time course pricing
- Comparable to Udemy Pro / Skillshare / LinkedIn Learning
- Conversion depends heavily on positioning and traffic source

### Premium ceiling

$997 - $2,997

- Cohort-based courses (CBC) with live elements
- High-context expertise (specialized B2B, regulated industry)
- Includes community access and limited Q&A

### Above $2,997

- Multi-month courses with deep community
- Specialized professional certifications
- Courses bundled with coaching layers

## Model 6: Subscription / membership

### Junior trap

$5 - $19/mo

- Newsletter Plus / Patreon tier territory
- Hard to scale to meaningful revenue solo (need 500+ subscribers at $19 for $10K/mo)
- Often becomes lifestyle expense for founder, not business

### Operational band

$29 - $99/mo

- Substantive subscription (membership site, premium newsletter, community)
- Targets 100-300 subscribers for meaningful revenue
- Requires sustained content cadence to retain

### Premium ceiling

$99 - $497/mo

- High-context community + content (Pavilion-style, On Deck-style)
- Includes some live element (monthly office hours, peer matching)
- Targets 50-150 subscribers for $5-50K MRR

### Above $497/mo

- Elite peer communities (YPO, EO membership-fee territory)
- High-touch consultation included
- Rare for solo operators

## Stage-aware recommendations

### Pre-Rev Builder

- Default to the OPERATIONAL band lower-mid range
- Use a 30-day money-back guarantee to de-risk for buyers
- Avoid the junior trap (it sets a long-term ceiling)
- Avoid the premium ceiling (you cannot sustain delivery at that price without track record)

Example: Solo coaching at $2,000-$3,000 for 90 days, with 30-day money-back.

### Early Operator (<$5K MRR)

- Validate v0.1.0 price against close rate
- If close rate above 50%, move up to mid-operational band
- If close rate below 20%, check positioning + ICP + offer scope before lowering price
- Test +15-20% increments quarterly

### Stable Solo ($5-25K MRR)

- Optimize within the operational band
- Consider a secondary offer at a different band (entry-level OR premium, not both)
- Begin tracking close rate per channel (cold vs. inbound vs. referral): prices may differ per channel
- Refund rate should be under 5%

### Scaling Solo ($25K+ MRR)

- Full tier ladder possible (operational band + premium ceiling)
- Test premium ceiling with deliberate positioning shifts (testimonials, brand assets, thought leadership)
- Track lifetime value per ICP segment
- Annual price reviews default to "raise unless reason not to"

## The 5x rule

A useful pricing heuristic:

**The seller should be earning 5x what they would charge per hour as a freelancer in the same domain.**

If a solo coach would charge $150/hr as a freelance coach, the productized engagement should produce $750/hr blended (when you divide engagement price by total hours, including delivery and prep and admin).

Why 5x:
- 1x = freelance hourly (no leverage)
- 2-3x = productized but underpriced
- 5x = productized at fair market value with positioning
- 10x = premium positioning with strong differentiation

For the example coaching business: $X,XXX / ~26 hours per engagement = $92/hr blended. Freelance coaching rate equivalent: ~$150/hr. Current ratio: 0.6x. UNDERPRICED.

But wait: $92/hr blended includes 30 minutes of admin/wk for 12 wks (6 hrs) + 30 min of email/text per wk (6 hrs) on top of the 12 hrs of calls = 24 hrs minimum. The 5x rule suggests Langley pricing should be $750/hr × 12 hrs of actual calls = $9,000 for the engagement.

That is the "premium ceiling" position. The current $X,XXX is in the operational band but at the LOWER end.

The pricing-strategy decision tree: test $3,200 (33% increase) with the next 5 prospects. If close rate stays above 30%, lock. If it drops, roll back. The ceiling at $9,000 may be reachable later with stronger positioning + outcome data + premium delivery additions.

## Common mistakes by stage

### Pre-Rev mistake: pricing too low to "get the first client"

The first client's price is a ceiling. If you start at $500, you cannot easily move to $2,500 with the same buyer-cohort. Pick the operational-band starting price and commit.

### Early Operator mistake: pricing too high before validation

The opposite mistake. The founder reads a course or book that says "charge premium" and prices at $10K before validating $2,500. Result: 6 months of crickets.

### Stable Solo mistake: not raising prices annually

If the user has been at the same price for 18+ months, they are losing real value to inflation + accumulated credibility. Raise 10-15% annually by default.

### Scaling Solo mistake: building tiers before validating one

The user has $30K MRR and builds Bronze / Silver / Gold tiers. The middle tier sells nothing. Bronze cannibalizes Gold. Net result: complexity without revenue gain.

Solution: validate one offer at one price. Add a SECOND offer only when the first is operationally stable and demand pattern justifies it.

## Currency notes

Default pricing assumes USD. Other currencies have different anchors:

- GBP: approximate 1:1 with USD for high-context premium positioning, otherwise slight discount
- EUR: similar to GBP
- CAD: 1.3x USD prices (e.g., USD $X,XXX ≈ CAD $3,200)
- AUD: 1.4x USD prices
- INR / BRL / other emerging market: locally-calibrated pricing required; do not directly convert from USD

Cross-border pricing: when selling globally, list price in USD as default, with a note "approximately X in [local currency]." Currency conversion at booking, not at quoting.

## Update triggers for these bands

These pricing bands shift over time:

- Inflation (2-4% annual default; review annually)
- Market saturation (more coaches → price pressure down)
- Market growth (more buyers → price pressure up)
- Specific economic events (recession → price compression; boom → price expansion)
- AI displacement (LLMs commoditize some advice categories → human-only premium expands)
