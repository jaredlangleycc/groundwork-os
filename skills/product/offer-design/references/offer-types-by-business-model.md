# Offer Types by Business Model

The 6-part offer design works across business models. The specifics adapt. This reference shows how each model expresses the 6 parts and what traps each model has.

---

## Model 1: Solo 1:1 coaching (the example coaching business pattern)

### Typical shape

Time-bound engagement (60-180 days). 1:1 with founder. Weekly cadence. Bounded async support. Productized so every client gets the same container.

### How the 6 parts express

1. **Deliverable:** 8-16 live coaching calls + a shared working document + async support
2. **Scope:** Coaching only. No therapy. No agency work. No advisor-of-record on the buyer's company.
3. **Timeline:** Fixed engagement length (commonly 90 days). Specific kickoff trigger. Specific end date.
4. **Price:** $1,500-$X,XXX range typical for 90-day engagements. Single price or 2-3 installments.
5. **Guarantee:** 30-day money-back is standard. Performance guarantees rare (outcome depends on buyer effort).
6. **Not for you:** No team or role to improve in. Aspirational shoppers. People wanting unlimited access.

### Traps to avoid

- Customizing every engagement (kills margins, slows sales cycle)
- Offering "unlimited Voxer / Slack" without business-hour bounds (eats the family floor)
- Going below 90 days for transformation work (too short for habit formation)
- Going above 180 days for first-time-buyer engagement (commitment is too big to close)
- Selling "ongoing" as the primary offer (better as the upsell after a fixed-length first engagement)

### Pricing notes

- Price-per-hour math: 90 days × 1 hr/wk = 12-13 hrs delivery + 5-10 hrs prep + admin = 17-23 hrs of seller time. At $X,XXX, that is $100-140/hr blended. Acceptable for Early Operator coaching.
- Don't sell hourly. The offer is the container, not the time inside it.

## Model 2: Group cohort / mastermind

### Typical shape

Cohort of 8-25 buyers. Time-bound (6-12 weeks). Live group sessions + community + curriculum.

### How the 6 parts express

1. **Deliverable:** N weekly live sessions (recorded) + Slack/Discord community + workbook + 1-2 office hours
2. **Scope:** Group only OR group + limited 1:1 (e.g., one 30-min onboarding call). Async community access during cohort dates only.
3. **Timeline:** Fixed start and end date. Cohort cadence (every quarter, twice a year).
4. **Price:** $500-$5,000 per seat depending on positioning and outcome. Pay-in-full or 2-3 installments.
5. **Guarantee:** Often a "first 2 sessions money back" guarantee (lets buyers de-risk without giving away the full cohort).
6. **Not for you:** Buyers who need 1:1 (route to a separate coaching offer). Buyers who cannot show up live (asynchronous is hard in cohorts).

### Traps to avoid

- Letting people "audit" or join mid-cohort (kills community + financial integrity)
- Running back-to-back cohorts without recovery time (founder burnout)
- Pricing cohort like 1:1 (cohort margin is higher; price reflects scarcity of seats and group dynamic, not per-hour)
- Offering "lifetime access" to recordings (creates support obligations indefinitely)
- Cohorts below 8 people (community dynamics break, energy drops)

### Pricing notes

- Higher attendance unit economics than 1:1. A 12-week cohort with 20 seats at $1,500 = $30K revenue. Founder delivery: 12 sessions × 90 min + prep = 30-40 hrs. Blended hourly: $750-1,000/hr.
- Cohort scarcity (8-25 seats per cohort, 2-4 cohorts per year) is what justifies premium pricing.

## Model 3: Productized service (fixed-scope deliverable)

### Typical shape

One-time deliverable. Fixed scope. Known output. Often packaged as a "sprint" or "audit" or "build."

### How the 6 parts express

1. **Deliverable:** A specific artifact (website redesign, brand audit, technical assessment, marketing playbook)
2. **Scope:** Detailed deliverable spec (pages, screens, sections, deliverable formats). Revision rounds limited (2 rounds standard).
3. **Timeline:** Fixed turnaround (e.g., 4 weeks). Specific milestones (kickoff, draft 1, draft 2, final).
4. **Price:** Fixed price for fixed scope. No hourly. Possible "scope additions" priced separately and quoted before work.
5. **Guarantee:** Often "If we fail to deliver milestone X by date Y, refund." Performance guarantees feasible (deliverable is concrete).
6. **Not for you:** Clients who want unlimited revisions. Clients who do not have content / data / inputs the deliverable requires.

### Traps to avoid

- "Hourly creep": letting fixed-scope blur into hourly billing
- Accepting "small additions" without re-quoting
- Not specifying the input requirements (e.g., "we cannot start the website until you provide brand assets, copy outline, and a complete asset inventory")
- Skipping revision-round caps (unlimited revisions = unlimited time = lost margin)
- Pricing by hours instead of by deliverable value

### Pricing notes

- Productized service margins are the highest of any model when scope is honest
- Time per delivery should decrease over time as the seller systematizes (templates, workflows)
- Same price per delivery even as cost-per-delivery drops = margin expansion

## Model 4: Retainer / fractional engagement

### Typical shape

Monthly recurring engagement. Founder embeds as fractional-role (CMO, CFO, CTO). Ongoing relationship, typically 6+ months.

### How the 6 parts express

1. **Deliverable:** A defined role (e.g., fractional CMO 10 hrs/wk) + specific monthly outputs (marketing plan, weekly leadership meetings, monthly board prep)
2. **Scope:** Specific responsibilities. Specific NOT-included (e.g., "I do not run your weekly all-hands; I attend monthly")
3. **Timeline:** Month-to-month with 30-day notice OR fixed 6-month commitment with renewal option. Engagement reviews quarterly.
4. **Price:** Monthly retainer. $3,000-$25,000/mo depending on role seniority + hours + outcome ownership.
5. **Guarantee:** Often 30-day exit clause (no refund of past months, but no future commitment).
6. **Not for you:** Companies that need a full-time hire. Companies that cannot give the fractional role real decision authority.

### Traps to avoid

- Hours-based retainer creep (10 hrs/wk becomes 25 hrs/wk silently)
- Taking equity in lieu of cash (almost always loses money for the fractional)
- Engaging without a defined exit (the retainer becomes a relationship the seller cannot end without drama)
- Not raising rates annually (inflation + scope creep + market shift make stale rates a margin trap)
- One client over 50% of revenue (concentration risk)

### Pricing notes

- Retainer math: $X/mo × 6 months = $6X total commitment value
- Compare to productized service: a $25K productized 4-week sprint can equal 4 months of $6K/mo retainer revenue with less ongoing commitment
- Retainers are good for predictable income but bad for scaling (capped at hours-per-week)

## Model 5: Course (self-paced, evergreen)

### Typical shape

Pre-recorded video curriculum + workbook + optional community. Buyer consumes asynchronously. No live touchpoints from founder (or very limited: monthly Q&A).

### How the 6 parts express

1. **Deliverable:** Video lessons (organized in modules) + workbook PDF + optional community access (e.g., 6 months)
2. **Scope:** Recorded content only. NO live coaching included. Community access bounded in time.
3. **Timeline:** Self-paced from purchase. Buyer access expires after N days OR never (lifetime).
4. **Price:** $97-$2,000 range typical. One-time or pay-in-3.
5. **Guarantee:** 7-day or 30-day money-back, no questions asked. Honor it.
6. **Not for you:** Buyers who need 1:1 (route to a coaching offer). Buyers who have not completed prerequisite skill levels.

### Traps to avoid

- Promising "lifetime access" then sunsetting the course later (legal + reputational risk)
- Selling courses to people who really need 1:1 coaching (course completion rates under 5% industry-wide)
- Pricing courses like coaching (price-anchor mismatch)
- No drip schedule (overwhelm; buyers consume nothing)
- No community + no support layer (delivery is too thin; refunds spike)

### Pricing notes

- Course margins are highest at scale (course built once, sold N times). But N has to be high.
- Cohort-based courses (CBC) hybrid the cohort + course models. Higher price, higher completion rate, higher support cost.

## Model 6: Hybrid (course + coaching ladder)

### Typical shape

Entry-level course ($) → mid-tier group cohort ($$) → premium 1:1 coaching ($$$). Buyer enters at low cost and graduates up.

### How the 6 parts express

This is THREE offers, not one. Each follows the 6-part design separately.

The ladder shape matters:
- Entry: low-risk, self-paced, designed to qualify buyers up
- Mid: cohort or group, designed to deepen and self-select
- Premium: 1:1, highest price, highest customization

The ladder requires operational discipline:
- Each tier has clear positioning vs. the others
- The upsell path is explicit (buyers know "after this, the next step is X")
- The founder can sustain all three (capacity matters)

### Traps to avoid

- Building all three before validating any one
- Inconsistent positioning across tiers (each tier sounds like a different brand)
- Upsell pressure that erodes trust (the entry course should not feel like a 5-hour sales pitch)
- Premium tier that does not produce 5x the outcome of the mid tier (the price differential should map to outcome differential)
- Skipping the cohort tier (going straight from course to 1:1 leaves a margin gap)

### Pricing notes

- Typical ladder ratios: $100 (course) → $1,500 (cohort) → $X,XXX (1:1): roughly 10-15x at each step
- Conversion rates between tiers: 5-15% course to cohort, 5-15% cohort to 1:1
- The ladder optimizes for lifetime value over single-offer revenue

## Cross-model offer design rules

These hold regardless of model:

### Rule 1: One offer per buyer at a time

A buyer should not have to choose between offers in the first conversation. Pick the right offer for their stage and present that one. (Exception: clear ladder with obvious upgrade path.)

### Rule 2: Scope creep is the killer

Every offer model has a different scope-creep failure mode. Build the not-included list specifically for the model's failure mode.

- 1:1: "no weekend sessions, no team coaching"
- Cohort: "no audits, no recording-only access"
- Productized: "no unlimited revisions, scope additions priced separately"
- Retainer: "no operational execution outside the named function"
- Course: "no 1:1 included, community access expires"
- Hybrid: "tier upgrades require new agreement"

### Rule 3: Guarantees match the model's risk profile

- 1:1 + cohort + retainer: 30-day money-back is standard, low refund risk
- Productized: milestone-based guarantee (refund if seller misses deadlines)
- Course: 7-30 day money-back, no questions asked
- Hybrid: each tier has its own guarantee

### Rule 4: Timeline must end (or have a clear renewal trigger)

Open-ended engagements kill margin and create relationship drag. Even retainers should have a quarterly review trigger.

### Rule 5: The pricing model fits the buyer's accounting frame

- B2B SaaS executives often prefer monthly retainer ("looks like a SaaS line item")
- Founders often prefer pay-in-full ("settled and forgotten")
- Solo professionals often prefer installments ("cash flow")

Ask the buyer (or know from `icp-discovery`). Build the structure to fit their accounting.

### Rule 6: The price band matches the perceived risk

If the buyer perceives high risk (new vendor, unknown outcome, expensive mistake), the price should be lower than the seller's "true" value AND the guarantee should be strong. Once trust is established, prices can move up the ladder.

## Choosing a model (decision tree)

Run through these in order:

### Q1: Do you have 10-15 hours/wk in the business?

- Yes → keep going
- No → simplify to Course or Productized only (less synchronous time required)

### Q2: Do you want to scale beyond 10 clients at any time?

- Yes → Cohort or Course (not 1:1)
- No → 1:1 or Productized

### Q3: Is the buyer's problem highly customized?

- Yes → 1:1 or Productized (not Course)
- No → Course or Cohort

### Q4: Does the buyer need real-time access?

- Yes → 1:1 or Cohort or Retainer
- No → Course or Productized

### Q5: Do you want recurring revenue?

- Yes → Retainer or Course (with subscription option) or Cohort (running 3-4x/year)
- No → 1:1 (fixed engagement) or Productized

The answers usually point to one or two models. Pick. Build one. Validate. Add a second model only after the first reaches $5K MRR.

## Model migration paths

### Pre-Rev → Early Operator → Stable Solo

Common migration: Productized → 1:1 → 1:1 + Course → Cohort + 1:1 ladder

### Coaching path

Common migration: 1:1 → Cohort → Course → ladder (all three tiers)

### Agency path

Common migration: Productized service → Retainer → Productized + Retainer ladder

Each migration is a re-run of `offer-design` for the new offer. Old offers may sunset or stay as ladder rungs.
