# ICP: the example coaching business

v1.0
Locked: 2026-05-18
Source: 7-question pass + 8 customer interviews + first 12 prospects in Accelerator funnel

---

## STAGE

- **Industry:** B2B SaaS (primary), B2B services (secondary)
- **Company stage:** Series A to Series C
- **Employee count:** 50 to 500
- **Revenue:** $5M to $50M ARR
- **Geography:** US, Canada, UK, Australia (English-speaking, similar work culture)
- **Funding:** Venture-backed (primary), self-funded with similar growth pace (secondary)

## ROLE

- **Title(s):** Sales Manager (primary), BDR Manager (primary), Frontline Sales Manager, Director of Sales (small teams)
- **Seniority:** First-line management (one layer above IC, reports to VP or Director)
- **Tenure in role:** 0 to 18 months
- **Decision power:** Often does not sign the contract alone. Influences. Will need VP or boss buy-in for the $X,XXX spend, but the buy is personal more than corporate. Personal credit card use is common.

## SITUATION

First-time or early-stage manager. Was a top IC. Got promoted because they were good at the IC job. Six months in, realizes the skills that got them promoted are not the skills the new role requires. The team is not closing. Their own number is not closing because they are now coaching reps instead of selling. They are doing 11 jobs at once and feel like they are doing all of them badly.

The specific moment they would describe to a peer: a Tuesday morning forecast call where they had to explain to their VP why the team missed quota, and they did not have a real answer beyond "the reps need to do better."

## PAIN

### Surface pain (what they would Google)

- "How to be a better sales manager"
- "First-time sales manager tips"
- "Sales manager coaching"
- "How to coach BDRs"
- "Why my sales team is not hitting quota"

### Real pain (what they would tell their spouse at 11pm)

- "I do not know what I am doing and I am scared they are going to figure it out."
- "I keep getting blamed for the team's misses but I do not have time to actually coach anyone."
- "I used to feel good at my job. Now I feel like a fraud."
- "I am working more hours than ever and I am less effective than I have ever been."

## JOB TO BE DONE

In their words:
> "I want to feel like I know what I am doing. I want a system I can trust so I am not making it up every day. I want my team to actually hit quota so I can stop bracing for the forecast call every Tuesday. And I want this to not eat my family time."

The outcome is confidence + a working system + protected time. Not "more sales training." They have had training. They want practice and accountability and someone who has seen this movie before.

## BUYING TRIGGER

### Primary trigger

Missed quota for the second consecutive quarter, plus a 1:1 with their boss where the boss said some version of: "We need to see something different next quarter." The buyer feels the role is at risk.

### Secondary trigger

Annual review or 360-feedback where the rating dropped. The buyer is publicly graded on something they thought they were doing well, and the rating contradicts their self-image. They start looking for help quietly.

### Tertiary trigger

New responsibility (team doubles in size, new product line, geographic expansion). The buyer feels the role expanded faster than their skill, and they want to get ahead of the next quarter before missing it.

## ANTI-ICP

### Stage anti-fit

- Solopreneurs with no team (no one to manage)
- Pre-seed founders without a sales team yet
- Enterprise C-suite executives (different problem, different price band)
- VPs of Sales with 5+ years of leadership experience (they need peer-level coaching, not first-time manager coaching)

### Role anti-fit

- Individual contributors (not yet promoted; pre-mature for this work)
- Customer Success managers (different functional context; the work would need to be adapted, and Langley has not built that variation)
- Marketing leaders, Product leaders (out of vertical)

### Situation anti-fit

- Companies in active acquisition or reorg (the buyer's role is unstable; coaching is the wrong investment)
- Companies in active fundraise (cash and attention go to the round, not to development)
- Layoffs in the last 90 days (psychological safety is gone; coaching cannot fix that)

### Pain anti-fit

- "I just need a thinking partner" without a real role or team to improve in (this is aspirational shopping; refund risk is high)
- "My team is fine, I am just looking for a peer to talk to" (no forcing function; will churn)
- "I want a coach so I can promote into VP" (career-coaching frame; Langley's work is role-execution, not career strategy)

### Trigger anti-fit

- No specific recent trigger ("I have been meaning to try coaching for years")
- Trigger from a personal life event only ("Just had a baby, want to be a better leader") without role context
- Trigger from external pressure they do not endorse ("My CEO told me I have to do this") without internal buy-in
