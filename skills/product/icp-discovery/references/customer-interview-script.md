# Customer Interview Script

Use during ICP discovery when the user cannot yet name 5 specific people who match their hypothesized ICP. Five to ten interviews with target ICP members produces enough signal to lock the ICP card.

This script is the free-tier version. No transcription tools required. The user does the listening. Notes go into the ICP card.

## Before the interview

### Pick interview targets in this order

1. **Past customers who fit well.** Most valuable, most likely to talk. Easy yes.
2. **Past customers who did NOT fit.** Defines anti-ICP. Painful to ask but high signal.
3. **Prospects who said no.** Defines objection patterns. Reach out gently.
4. **Peers in adjacent roles at target ICP companies.** Defines language patterns and triggers. Cold-friendly.

### Reach out (free channels)

The ask: 5 questions, 30 minutes, recorded with permission, exchanged for nothing (or a $25 gift card if budget allows). Most people say yes if the ask is small and the framing is research-not-pitch.

Sample LinkedIn DM:

```
Hi [Name],

I am researching how [specific role, e.g., first-time sales managers] navigate [specific challenge, e.g., the first 90 days in the role]. I am not selling anything. I am trying to understand the role better so I can write about it accurately.

Would you be open to 30 minutes on Zoom for me to ask you 5 questions about your experience? I will record (with your permission) for note-taking only. No outreach after.

If yes, here is my calendar: [link]. If no or not now, no offense.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

Sample email (to past customer):

```
Hi [Name],

I am refining how I describe the work I do so it lands with the right people. Your engagement was one of the ones I learned the most from.

Would you give me 30 minutes for 5 questions about what you were going through when we first talked? Just research. No upsell. I will record for my notes only.

Calendar here: [link]. Thanks for considering.
[Your name]
```

### Setup

- 30 minutes blocked
- Recording on (with permission)
- Notebook + pen for live notes
- This script open in a tab
- One follow-up question per main question (do not script more than that, let it flow)

## The 10-question script

### Setup (1 minute)

> "Thanks for the time. I am [Your name] and I work with [ICP description]. I am researching the role to understand it better. I have five questions and may have one or two follow-ups. Recording for my notes only. Anything you say off the record is off the record. Ready?"

### Question 1: Role + tenure (warm-up, 2 minutes)

> "Tell me about your role and how long you have been in it."

What to listen for:
- Title vs. actual job
- Tenure (the buying trigger often correlates with tenure)
- Surprises about the role they did not expect

Follow-up if needed:
> "What was different about the role than you expected when you took it?"

### Question 2: Today's hardest part (5 minutes)

> "What is the hardest part of your day-to-day right now?"

What to listen for:
- Surface pain (what they would Google)
- Real pain (what they would tell their spouse at 11pm)
- The language THEY use (not the language you use about them)

Follow-up if needed:
> "When was the last time that hit you? What did you do?"

This question often surfaces the buying trigger in disguise.

### Question 3: Last 90 days (3 minutes)

> "What is the hardest part of the last 90 days specifically?"

Why 90 days: it forces specificity. "In general" answers stay vague. "In the last 90 days" pulls real moments.

What to listen for:
- Specific incidents (the one client meeting that went sideways, the one quarter that missed)
- The pattern they keep hitting (the same wall, multiple times)

### Question 4: Where they have turned (4 minutes)

> "When you have hit walls before, who or what have you turned to? Why them?"

What to listen for:
- The alternatives they tried (your competition, including DIY, including a peer, including silence)
- What worked and what did not
- The criteria they used to pick

This is gold for positioning. The "unlike [alternative] which [shortcoming]" line comes from this answer.

### Question 5: The trigger (4 minutes)

> "Was there a specific moment in the last year that made you seek help? What was happening?"

What to listen for:
- The forcing function (boss pressure, public miss, identity crisis, promotion, new responsibility)
- The exact moment they decided to act
- Who else was in the room

The buying trigger fires on a moment, not a state. Pin the moment.

Follow-up if needed:
> "What was the conversation that made you actually start looking for help?"

### Question 6: Magic wand (3 minutes)

> "If you had a magic wand, what would be different in 90 days?"

What to listen for:
- The outcome they hire FOR, in their language
- The dream vs. the floor (separate the bold from the bare minimum)
- The metric they would use to know it worked

Follow-up if needed:
> "What would your boss say if it worked?"
> "What would you tell your spouse if it worked?"

### Question 7: What did not work (3 minutes)

> "What have you tried in this area that did not work? Why did it fail?"

What to listen for:
- Objection patterns (the reasons they said no to past options)
- The "I tried X and it sucked" pattern (your differentiator hides here)
- Sunk cost stories (they spent money or time on something that disappointed)

### Question 8: Wish they had known (2 minutes)

> "What do you wish someone had told you when you started this role?"

What to listen for:
- The lesson learned the hard way (your content marketing hides here)
- The advice they would give a peer (your headline language hides here)

### Question 9: The price test (3 minutes)

> "If you were going to spend money on a coach or consultant or tool to help with this, what would it have to do for you to say yes? And what would your budget realistically be?"

What to listen for:
- Their willingness-to-pay (rough number)
- The proof they would need (testimonials, case studies, free trial, money-back)
- The decision-maker (themselves, their boss, their finance team)

This question is delicate. Lead with "I am not pitching you" framing. The answer informs pricing-strategy and offer-design.

### Question 10: Who else (1 minute)

> "Who else should I talk to who is in a similar spot?"

What to listen for:
- Names. Get specific. Ask for intros.

If they hesitate, lower the ask:
> "Anyone in your network you think I should listen to? No intro needed, just names."

This question is the multiplier. Five interviews can become 15 if you ask Question 10 every time.

Within 24 hours, write notes against this template:

```
INTERVIEW NOTES: [Name, Company, Role]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Duration: [N minutes]
Recording: [path or "no recording"]

ROLE + TENURE
[Their answer to Q1, plus follow-up]

PAIN (surface)
[Their answer to Q2, in their words]

PAIN (real / private)
[The version they would tell their spouse, if surfaced]

TRIGGER MOMENT
[Specific moment from Q3 or Q5]

ALTERNATIVES TRIED
- [Alternative 1]: [why it worked / failed]
- [Alternative 2]: [why it worked / failed]

DESIRED OUTCOME
[Their answer to Q6, in their words]

OBJECTIONS / FAILURES
[Their answer to Q7]

LANGUAGE PATTERNS
- [Phrase 1 they used that you want to mirror]
- [Phrase 2]
- [Phrase 3]

- [Name 1]
- [Name 2]

NOTES + QUOTES
[Verbatim quotes you want to remember]
```

Save to `docs/customer-interviews/<date>-<name>.md`.

## Pattern recognition (after 5+ interviews)

Open all the interview notes side-by-side. Look for:

### Repeated language

If three interviewees independently say "I feel like I am winging it," that phrase belongs in your copy.

If five interviewees say "I do not have time for another tool," that objection belongs in your sales materials.

### Repeated triggers

If four out of five interviewees mentioned a boss conversation as the trigger, lead outreach with that.

If three out of five mentioned a public miss (missed quota, lost a client), use that frame.

### Repeated alternatives

If everyone tried Course X and it did not work, your positioning differentiator is clear: "Unlike [Course X] which gives you frameworks but no accountability, this gives you weekly check-ins."

### Repeated price points

If four people give willingness-to-pay in the $2,000-3,500 range, that is your pricing band.

If two people say "I would pay $20K for this if it actually worked," explore: did they buy at that price elsewhere? What was the difference?

### Repeated names referred

If multiple interviewees name the same other person, that person is influential in your ICP. Reach out to them last (after you have shown up well to 5+ others) for maximum credibility.

## What NOT to do during interviews

### Do not pitch

The interview is research. The moment you pitch, the interview collapses into a sales conversation and you lose the signal. If they ask what you do, give a one-sentence answer and route back to the question.

> "I work with [ICP] on [outcome]. Not selling today, though. I am just trying to understand the role better. Back to your last point..."

### Do not lead the witness

Do not phrase questions in a way that suggests the answer.

Bad: "Most managers tell me they feel overwhelmed. Do you feel that?"
Good: "What is the hardest part of your day right now?"

### Do not interrupt to correct or expand

Even when the interviewee says something you think is wrong, let it stand. The wrong answer is data. Their wrong answer is YOUR copy opportunity.

### Do not skip Question 10

It is the highest-leverage question. Five interviews can multiply to 15 with one ask per call.

### Do not run interviews longer than 30 minutes

Respect the time. Sticking to 30 minutes builds reputation. People will recommend you to others if you were brief.

---

## When to stop interviewing

After 5-10 interviews, you should hit pattern saturation. New interviews produce no new patterns. Stop. Lock the ICP card.

If after 10 interviews you still cannot lock the ICP, the ICP hypothesis was too broad. Pick the narrowest subset of the 10 (the 2-3 who fit best) and try again. Or accept the ICP is wider than expected and split into 2 ICPs (only if MRR is $25K+).

---

## When to re-interview

Quarterly check-in. Or:

- After a pivot
- After a price change
- After visible market shift (industry layoff, funding shift, regulatory change)
- When close rate drops without obvious cause
- When new objections start appearing in discovery calls

Each new interview cohort produces a v1.1 of the ICP card.
