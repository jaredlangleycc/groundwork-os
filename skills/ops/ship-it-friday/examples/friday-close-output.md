## What this log models

### Step 1 surfaces decisions the user did not log mid-week

The "Pass on Acme partnership" decision was added during the close-out. Without ship-it-friday, the "said no" decision would never be logged because it is the easiest decision to forget. The log captures it.

### Step 2 file-sweep is one line, full log linked

Ship-it-friday does not duplicate file-sweep's output. One summary line, one link, move on.

### Step 3 week recap is category-grouped

Content, Sales, Client delivery, Ops. Each category has the week's items. Future-Jared reading this knows what shipped in each lane without scrolling through 30 line items.

### Step 4 family-floor specifies what was overridden

The Wed 4:30-5:15pm override has full context (Alex, Sarah notified, make-up plan). Pattern flag check is explicit. No moralizing.

### Step 5 revenue-mapper output shows the mix and the floor compliance

The plan has 12 items. Each is tagged. The mix is computed. The floor is 69% (above the 65% Early Operator floor). The check is explicit; the user can see the compliance.

### Step 6 finance-log on last Friday

The full monthly finance ritual ran. Burn, income, runway, trend, stale subs, YTD, concentration, pricing pulse, honest mirror. All in the log.

### Step 7 closing question is specific to the gap

The largest gap surfaced is the pricing pulse: 30 days at $X,XXX with no sales at the new price, and Alex is the first prospect at the new price. The closing question targets the kill-signal logic. It does not list five questions. It does not soften.
