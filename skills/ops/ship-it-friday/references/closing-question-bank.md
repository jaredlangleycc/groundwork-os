# Closing Question Bank

The closing reflection at the end of every Friday close-out. The skill picks one question based on the largest gap surfaced. The bank prevents the question from becoming rote.

## How the bank is organized

Questions are grouped by the gap that surfaces them. Each group has 3-5 questions for variety. The skill rotates through the group so the same Friday does not get the same question 6 weeks running.

## Gap: $$$ below floor (revenue mix is light)

### Q1
What is the activity below the line you would not do, and what would you do instead?

### Q2
Of the $$ activities you logged for next week, which one converts into $$$ in 30 days or fewer? Pull that one forward.

### Q3
You have 3 days inside the floor next week. Pick one and move it to direct revenue. Which?

### Q4
Was the $$ overweight this week because the $$$ was scary, or because the $$$ was not available?

### When NOT to ask

If the user is mid-delivery on a $5K+ project and the "low $$$ next week" is delivery cost on an already-won deal, this gap is a false positive. Surface that and skip the question:

> "$$$ next week looks low, but you are mid-delivery on Sam + Jordan. Delivery hours are $$$ that show as $ in the mapping. Re-tag and re-check."

## Gap: Family floor pattern flag tripped

### Q1
What changed in your life that the floor has not caught up to?

### Q2
The override count says the floor is not the floor anymore. Rewrite it or renew it. Which?

### Q3
4 overrides in 30 days. Pick the one you would do again. Pick the one you regret. What is the pattern between them?

### Q4
Your stated floor is M-F 4-7pm. Your actual floor is closer to M-F 5-7pm. Confirm the new floor or rebuild the old one. Pick.

### When NOT to ask

If the user has been honest about a temporary phase (launch week, year-end push) and the overrides were planned, surface that and shift to a different question:

> "Overrides this month were planned, not drift. Skip the floor question. What is the next phase that has a floor that holds?"

## Gap: Stale drafts piling (4+ over 14 days)

### Q1
The drafts are not the bottleneck. What is?

### Q2
Pick one draft to ship Sunday. Pick two to archive. Pick zero to keep waiting.

### Q3
What does it mean about the work that you cannot finish it but cannot let it go?

### Q4
Six drafts at 70%. Pick the one with the strongest single sentence. Ship the sentence as a Threads post; mothball the rest.

### When NOT to ask

If the user is on a planned pause for content (sabbatical, focus quarter), surface:

> "Stale drafts noted but content is paused per CLAUDE.md > Goals. Park the question, run again at unpause."

## Gap: Pipeline shrinking 2 weeks running

### Q1
What is the prospect you have been letting cool because you do not want the answer?

### Q2
The pipeline shrunk this week and last. The next move you have been avoiding is what?

### Q3
Pick a name from the dead-leads list. Restart Monday. The deal does not come back to life by itself.

### Q4
Last week's pipeline projected X. This week's projects Y. The gap is real. What is the math?

### When NOT to ask

If the user is intentionally narrowing pipeline (qualifying out, focusing on fewer larger deals), surface:

> "Pipeline shrink is intentional this quarter (fewer, larger deals per CLAUDE.md > Goals). Park the count, ask the larger-deal question instead: which one is closing this month, and what is the kill signal if it does not?"

## Gap: Stage misfires unresolved (crm-hygiene ran, misfires not fixed)

### Q1
Of the won-no-income deals, which one is real and which is the lie you are telling the CRM?

### Q2
Two stage misfires were logged this morning. Pick one to resolve before close-out ends.

### Q3
The CRM is a story. Stage misfires are the chapters that do not match. Pick a chapter to fix.

## Gap: Zero sales activity this week (no calls, no outreach, no proposals)

### Q1
What did selling cost you this week that the other work did not?

### Q2
You went 5 days without a sales touch. What was the next-task that kept you from outreach?

### Q3
Selling was the work you did not do this week. The week wanted that work. What does next week want?

### When NOT to ask

If the user is mid-delivery and has explicitly paused outreach (per CLAUDE.md > Sales Motion or a logged decision), surface:

> "Outreach paused this week per [decision log entry]. Park the question. Confirm: when does outreach resume?"

## Gap: Strong week, no major gaps

### Q1
What is the one thing that, if it shipped next week, would make this week feel worth the floor breaks?

### Q2
A clean week is rare. What was the structural change that made it clean, and how do you preserve it?

### Q3
Strong weeks are the ones where you remember the work was the work. What was the one moment this week you wish would happen more often?

### Q4
Nothing flagging means the system is working or the system is not detecting. Which? What would a real failure look like next week so you would catch it?

## Gap: Finance check (last Friday of month) surfaces a question

### Q1 (revenue flat)
The skill said revenue is flat for the third month. What is the leverage move you have been putting off?

### Q2 (burn rising)
Burn ticked up $30 this month. Was that the upgrade that earns its keep, or operational comfort?

### Q3 (kill candidates surfaced)
You have $336 in annual subscription kill candidates. Pick one to cancel before close-out ends.

### Q4 (pricing pulse triggered)
The new price has been live 30 days with zero sales at the new price. What is the kill signal you set on the new price?

## Gap: Quarter close

### Q1
What was the bet you made 12 weeks ago, and what did it return?

### Q2
Three quarters in your year. This one is closing. Honest mirror: was it a real quarter or a recovery quarter?

### Q3
The quarter taught you something the prior one did not. Name the thing. The next quarter will test whether you learned it.

## Gap: Year close

### Q1
What pattern from this year did you not see clearly until the end? What is the first move for that pattern in January?

### Q2
You are 12 months older. The business is also 12 months older. Which one grew more?

### Q3
The year had one decision you made faster than usual that turned out right. Name it. The year had one decision you sat on too long. Name that too. What's the lesson the comparison shows?

## Gap: Multi-week drift (no Friday close-out for 3+ weeks)

### Q1
3 weeks without a close-out. What broke the rhythm, and what does Monday look like if the rhythm does not return?

### Q2
The skill is back. You are back. What is the smallest commitment that holds the rhythm this time?

### Q3
You missed 3 closes. Either the ritual is not worth the friction or the friction is not the ritual. Which?

## Custom question for the user's coach archetype

The skill calibrates the closing question to the user's coach archetype (from CLAUDE.md > Coach Style):

### Challenger archetype

Lead with the harder version of the bank's question. Skip soft hedges.

### Steady-Hand archetype

Lead with the reflective version. More space.

### Drill-Sergeant archetype

The question can be blunt. The user opted in.

### Reflective archetype

Often the question becomes "sit with this for a day" instead of "answer this now."

## Rotation rule

The skill keeps a record of which question fired in the last 8 Fridays (stored in CLAUDE.md > Skills > ship-it-friday > question_history). If the same gap surfaces this week as 4 weeks ago, the skill picks a different question from the bank.

This prevents the ritual from becoming rote without producing a totally different ritual each time.

## When the closing reflection is heavier than usual

Some Fridays the gap is severe (4-week absence, pattern flag tripped twice in 60 days, revenue collapse). In those cases:

1. Surface the gap explicitly.
2. Pick the question.
3. Add: "This is a sit-with question, not an answer-now question. Sleep on it through the weekend."

The skill does not soften the gap, but it gives the user permission to take the question into the weekend instead of forcing an answer Friday evening.

## What the closing reflection does NOT do

- Does not give advice.
- Does not propose multiple paths.
- Does not stack multiple questions.
- Does not soften with "if you want to think about it."
- Does not include a smiley or any emoji.
- Does not promise the user will figure it out.

One question. The user owns it.
