# Floor Archetypes

Common protected-time shapes for working-parent solopreneurs. Use these as starting points when the user's CLAUDE.md floor block is vague. Always rewrite to specifics.

## Dual-income with young kids (under 10)

The most common NightShiftOS archetype. One or both partners have a day job. Kids need bedtime, breakfast, weekend transport.

Typical floor:

- M-F 4:30pm to 8pm (pickup, dinner, bedtime)
- Saturday until 10am (slow morning)
- Sunday all day (family day)

Override pressure points:

- Client calls in the 4pm to 5pm window (last meeting of a US workday)
- Saturday morning "just one quick thing"
- Sunday evening "Sunday scaries planning session"

Hold the line on: Sunday. Sunday is the highest-leverage block to hold because it sets the rhythm for the week.

## Dual-income with older kids (10 to 17)

Kids are less hands-on but more emotionally available-time-dependent. Sports schedules, school events, college conversations.

Typical floor:

- M-F 5pm to 8pm (dinner + presence)
- One sport-specific block (e.g., Tuesday 6-9pm for kid's game)
- Sunday morning until noon

Override pressure points:

- Kid's game-night, work calls overlapping
- College tour weekends
- Sport away-games eating the Saturday floor

Hold the line on: any block named with the kid's specific activity. "Saturday Henry's soccer" is sticky. "Saturday morning" drifts.

## Single-parent

The floor is harder and the override cost is higher. No partner to absorb floor breaks.

Typical floor:

- All times kid is in user's primary custody and awake
- Custody-swap days as transition blocks (no work the morning the kid arrives, no work the morning after the kid leaves)
- One night per custody week as personal-recovery (yoga, friends, therapy)

Override pressure points:

- Day-job emergencies forcing custody arrangement breaks
- Side-business client emergencies during awake-kid hours

Hold the line on: personal-recovery night. Single-parent burnout collapses when the recovery block goes first.

## Partner with shift work

One partner has fixed evenings (most common: nurse, first responder, pilot, restaurant shift). The other's floor flips around the shift.

Typical floor:

- All evenings the shift-partner is home (often 3-4 nights a week)
- Whichever weekend day the shift-partner has off (usually one, sometimes none)
- Pre-shift transition window if shift-partner needs morning prep

Override pressure points:

- Days the partner's shift cancels last-minute (floor opens, user is tempted to fill it)
- Partner's vacation weeks (floor is full-on family time)

Hold the line on: floor opens are not work-windows. They are bonus family time.

## Religious observance

Friday sundown to Saturday sundown (Shabbat). Sunday morning service. Ramadan iftar windows. Sabbath days vary by tradition.

Typical floor:

- Sabbath day(s), full block, no work
- Religious-community events (one weeknight per week minimum)
- Pre-service prep (Sunday morning until service ends + lunch)

Override pressure points:

- Sabbath emails ("just looking, not responding")
- Service-day "just one client text"

Hold the line on: the digital boundary inside the sabbath. The body off the laptop is most of the gain.

## Recovery-specific blocks

Therapy, AA, gym, weekly run group, somatic practice. These are non-negotiable health blocks.

Typical floor:

- 90 minutes per week minimum, recurring same-day same-time
- Travel time to and from the block (do not eat the transition)
- Some users add "no client calls in the 4 hours after therapy"

Override pressure points:

- Skipping the block "this week only" three weeks running
- Booking calls in the post-therapy recovery window

Hold the line on: recurring weekly. The recovery practice loses its effect the moment it becomes situational.

## Partner-owned blocks

Date night. Anniversary. Partner's birthday week. Partner's solo travel week (where the at-home partner picks up extra family load).

Typical floor:

- Date night, fixed weekly, often Friday or Saturday evening
- Partner's birthday week (lighter business commitments)
- Partner's solo travel weeks (zero evening work)

Override pressure points:

- "Quick" Friday evening calls before date night
- Anniversary weekend "I'll just check email Saturday morning"

Hold the line on: the partner notices. The partner remembers. Partner-owned blocks have the longest tail when broken.

## Single-floor (no partner, no kids)

Some NightShiftOS users are solo. The floor still matters because solo founders burn out without protected recovery.

Typical floor:

- One full weekend day, picked by the user, recurring
- One weeknight per week (often Friday)
- One morning per week, recurring (often Sunday until 11am)

Override pressure points:

- "Nobody is waiting for me" mid-evening drift
- Weekend "I'll just work because I want to" trap

Hold the line on: the recurring weeknight. Solo founders without one fixed off-night lose the rhythm of having a non-work life.

## How to phrase the floor in CLAUDE.md

Bad (vague, drifts):

- "Evenings with family"
- "Weekends mostly off"
- "Some recovery time"

Good (specific, holds):

- "M-F 4:30pm to 8pm, no work, no exceptions"
- "Saturday all day with kids, partner gets Sunday morning until noon"
- "Wednesday 6-9pm yoga + recovery, no calls 9pm-10pm cooldown"

Specificity is the difference between a floor that holds and a floor that drifts. When in doubt, write the boundary with the actual clock time and the actual day. Vague floors lose the override battle every time.
